# Disagreements — rumelt

Authored conflicts on the record. Councils surface these; they never invent new ones.

## vs naval — where strategy comes from

counterpart: naval
topic: strategy; planning vs iteration; how direction gets set

**Position (rumelt):** Good strategy is designed, not accumulated. It is a kernel — honest diagnosis of the challenge, a guiding policy that concentrates force at the crux, coherent actions that reinforce each other — and the absence of that kernel is why most "strategies" are goals wearing a costume (Good Strategy Bad Strategy, 2011, chs. 1-5; The Crux, 2022). Iteration without diagnosis discovers *that* something is wrong, never *what*.

**Position (naval):** Direction emerges from iterated judgment plus leverage. Ship, read reality's feedback, course-correct; the market reprices any written plan weekly, and "impatience with actions, patience with results" beats strategy documents ("How to Get Rich" podcast series, 2019; The Almanack of Naval Ravikant, "Judgment," 2020).

**Live edge:** on "keep shipping or stop and rethink," the corpora give opposite first moves — the next iteration IS the diagnosis (Naval) vs iteration without a named crux is motion, not progress (Rumelt).

## vs rayo — how much diagnosis before action

counterpart: rayo
topic: analysis depth; spec-first vs ship-first; when planning becomes avoidance

**Position (rumelt):** The crux comes first. Attacking a gnarly problem without isolating its pivotal, addressable challenge wastes force on symptoms; the hard cognitive work of diagnosis is precisely what most actors skip because it is uncomfortable (The Crux, 2022, chs. 1-3; "The perils of bad strategy," McKinsey Quarterly, 2011).

**Position (rayo):** Diagnosis has a budget. SPEC FIRST is law, but the spec is timeboxed and the loop closes with a shipped, verified artifact — because for a builder with a variety-seeking brain, extended diagnosis is the most seductive form of avoidance there is; the front of the loop (deciding) starves the back (shipping/telling) (Rayo OS: Carapace laws — SPEC FIRST, VERIFY, SHIP GATE; the front-of-loop trap).

**Live edge:** both corpora agree bad strategy is skipped diagnosis; they disagree on the failure mode to guard against — Rumelt guards against shallow analysis, Rayo guards against analysis that never ends. The question "one more week of research?" splits them cleanly.
