## On muscle and aging (verified verbatim)

> "Never in the history of human civilization has a 90-year-old said, 'I wish I had less muscle.'"
— *Outlive* (2023), on the strength pillar; quoted on peterattiamd.com. Verified verbatim.

> "By age eighty, the average person will have lost eight kilograms of muscle, or about eighteen pounds, from their peak."
— *Outlive* (2023). Verified verbatim (independent transcription).

## On VO2 max and mortality (verified verbatim)

> "It is more strongly associated with reduced mortality risk than any other metric we know of, and this is likely because it's the best integral function we have for exercise volume and intensity."

## Core positions (paraphrase — not verbatim)

The following capture the corpus's positions in paraphrase. They are **[paraphrase]** and must not be presented as direct quotations.

- **[paraphrase]** Longevity is the balance of lifespan (how long you live) and healthspan (how long you live in good health); the goal is both, not just more years (*Outlive*, ch. 1).
- **[paraphrase]** Medicine 2.0 treats disease reactively once it appears; Medicine 3.0 is proactive, personalized, honest about risk, and weights healthspan equally (*Outlive*, ch. 3).
- **[paraphrase]** Most deaths in non-smokers over 50 come from four disease categories — the Four Horsemen: atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, cancer, neurodegenerative disease, and metabolic dysfunction (*Outlive*, ch. 3).
- **[paraphrase]** In biology, the best we can hope for is to reduce our uncertainty, not to reach certainty (*Outlive*, ch. 3).
- **[paraphrase]** The claim that one perfect diet works for every single person is incorrect; nutrition must be personalized to the individual's biochemistry (*Outlive*, ch. 15).
- **[paraphrase]** Attia now spends about as much time on his emotional health as on exercise, and believes that is as it should be, because physical and emotional health are closely linked (*Outlive*, ch. 17).
- **[paraphrase]** Exogenous molecules — drugs, hormones, supplements — are the last of the five tactical domains, deployed after exercise, nutrition, sleep, and emotional health and graded by evidence (*Outlive*, ch. 3; Drive AMA #35, 2022).
