# Sources — Robert Cialdini

Complete bibliography with dates. Primary sources (Cialdini's own books and papers) first, then the foundational experiments the corpus relies on, then applied field studies. Every claim in this seat traces to an entry below.

## Primary — books

- **Cialdini, R. B. (1984 / 2021).** *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion.* Original edition 1984 (rev. 2007); **New & Expanded edition**, Harper Business, 2021 (ISBN 978-0-06-293765-0). Classic-edition chapters: 1 **Weapons of Influence**; 2 Reciprocation; 3 Commitment & Consistency; 4 Social Proof; 5 **Liking**; 6 **Authority**; 7 Scarcity; 8 Instant Influence. The 2021 edition **retitles ch. 1 "Levers of Influence"**, renames the "click, whirr" metaphor **"click, run"**, and elevates **Unity** to the seventh principle (ch. 8) — Unity having first been introduced in *Pre-Suasion* (2016), not invented in 2021.
- **Cialdini, R. B. (2016).** *Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade.* Simon & Schuster (ISBN 978-1-5011-0979-9). Introduces the privileged moment, channeled attention, "what's focal is causal," the **Unity** concept (ch. 12), and the ethics of influence (cost of dishonesty to organizations).
- **Cialdini, R. B. (2018).** "The Influence Smugglers." *Psychology Today* (March 2018). The bungler / smuggler / sleuth (detective) typology of influence practitioners, with the quoted definitions used in this seat.
- **Goldstein, N. J., Martin, S. J., & Cialdini, R. B. (2008).** *Yes! 50 Scientifically Proven Ways to Be Persuasive.* Free Press (US) / Profile Books (UK). Fifty short, evidence-based persuasion tactics organized loosely around the principles.

## Primary — Cialdini's peer-reviewed papers

- **Cialdini, R. B., Vincent, J. E., Lewis, S. K., Catalan, J., Wheeler, D., & Darby, B. L. (1975).** "Reciprocal concessions procedure for inducing compliance: The door-in-the-face technique." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 31*(2), 206-215.
- **Cialdini, R. B., Cacioppo, J. T., Bassett, R., & Miller, J. A. (1978).** "Low-ball procedure for producing compliance: Commitment then cost." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 36*(5), 463-476.
- **Cialdini, R. B., Reno, R. R., & Kallgren, C. A. (1990).** "A focus theory of normative conduct: Recycling the concept of norms to reduce littering in public places." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 58*(6), 1015-1026. (Origin of the descriptive vs. injunctive norm distinction.)
- **Cialdini, R. B. (2003).** "Crafting normative messages to protect the environment." *Current Directions in Psychological Science, 12*(4), 105-109. (The Petrified Forest National Park sign study: injunctive-norm sign 1.67% theft vs. descriptive-norm sign 7.92%.)
- **Cialdini, R. B. (2001).** "Harnessing the science of persuasion." *Harvard Business Review*, October 2001, 72-79 (reprint R0109D). Managerial digest of the six principles.

## Foundational experiments the corpus builds on (cited *by* Cialdini)

- **Regan, D. T. (1971).** "Effects of a favor and liking on compliance." *Journal of Experimental Social Psychology, 7*(6), 627-639. (The unsolicited-Coke reciprocity study; basis of the reciprocity principle.)
- **Freedman, J. L., & Fraser, S. C. (1966).** "Compliance without pressure: The foot-in-the-door technique." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 4*(2), 195-202. (Not Cialdini's paper; the empirical basis of the commitment/consistency principle.)
- **Langer, E. J., Blank, A., & Chanowitz, B. (1978).** "The mindlessness of ostensibly thoughtful action: The role of 'placebic' information in interpersonal interaction." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 36*(6), 635-642. (The copy-machine "because" study; the "click, run" shortcut.)
- **Milgram, S. (1963, 1974).** Obedience-to-authority experiments; *Obedience to Authority* (1974). Cited by Cialdini as the extreme case of deference to authority (ch. 6, "Authority").
- **Iyengar, S. S., & Lepper, M. R. (2000).** "When choice is demotivating: Can one desire too much of a good thing?" *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 79*(6), 995-1006. (The jam-tasting study; cited in *Yes!* on choice overload.)

## Applied / field studies (Cialdini and colleagues)

- **Goldstein, N. J., Cialdini, R. B., & Griskevicius, V. (2008).** "A room with a viewpoint: Using social norms to motivate environmental conservation in hotels." *Journal of Consumer Research, 35*(3), 472-482. (Provincial norms; towel-reuse field experiments.)
- **Schultz, P. W., Nolan, J. M., Cialdini, R. B., Goldstein, N. J., & Griskevicius, V. (2007).** "The constructive, destructive, and reconstructive power of social norms." *Psychological Science, 18*(5), 429-434. (Household-energy boomerang effect; the injunctive-cue remedy.)
- **Burger, J. M. (1986).** "Increasing compliance by improving the deal: The that's-not-all technique." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 51*(2), 277-283. (The that's-not-all technique; discussed by Cialdini, but the key experiments are Burger's.)
- **Strohmetz, D. B., Rind, B., Fisher, R., & Lynn, M. (2002).** "Sweetening the till: The use of candy to increase restaurant tipping." *Journal of Applied Social Psychology, 32*(2), 300-309. (Unexpected-gift reciprocity; cited in *Yes!*)

## Verification notes

- Journal volumes, issues, and page ranges for the five Cialdini papers, plus Regan (1971), Freedman & Fraser (1966), Langer et al. (1978), Burger (1986), Goldstein et al. (2008), and Schultz et al. (2007), were confirmed against indexing records during authoring.
- Unity was introduced in *Pre-Suasion* (2016) and elevated to the seventh principle in the 2021 *Influence* edition (six principles to seven) — a documented edition difference; pre-2021 *Influence* material and the HBR 2001 article reference six principles only. The chapter-1 retitling ("Weapons" → "Levers") and the "click, whirr" → "click, run" change are likewise 2021-edition differences.
- Where a claim in this seat could not be tied to a verifiable primary source, it was paraphrased and flagged, or omitted, rather than attached to a fabricated citation.
