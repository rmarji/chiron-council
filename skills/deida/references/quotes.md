# Quotes — David Deida

Short attributed excerpts only, per the depth bar: wording checked against the source where possible; items whose exact wording could not be verified are explicitly marked as paraphrase or as widely reproduced. Use sparingly, always with attribution, and keep application non-explicit.

## Verified excerpts

- "In any given moment, a man's growth is optimized if he leans just beyond his edge, his capacity, his fear." — The Way of the Superior Man (1997), Part One, "Lean Just Beyond Your Edge." The corpus's dosage formula for growth: not comfort, not recklessness, the lean.

- "Every man knows that his highest purpose in life cannot be reduced to any particular relationship." — The Way of the Superior Man, Part One, "Your Purpose Must Come Before Your Relationship." The opening claim of the purpose-first teaching.

- "The masculine grows by challenge, but the feminine grows by praise." — The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two, "Praise Her." The asymmetry behind the praise-over-criticism framework.

## Paraphrases (marked as such — not verbatim)

- Paraphrase: a man should give his deepest gift now, rather than waiting for fear to subside, because the fear does not subside first — condensed from "Lean Just Beyond Your Edge" and "Do It for Love" (The Way of the Superior Man, Part One).
- Paraphrase: the feminine partner does not want a perfect man; she wants a man who is fully present with her — condensed from Dear Lover (2005) and The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two. Frequently circulated as a direct quote in several wordings; none verified here.
- Paraphrase: "your woman is not your fault" circulates from Deida's workshop teaching to mean a partner's moods are weather to be met with presence, not a problem you caused or must fix; wording unverified against a published text, so treat as reported workshop language (Deida workshops and recorded talks; consistent with The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two).
- Paraphrase: emotional statements made in a storm are the truth of that moment, not testimony about facts or the future — condensed from "Women Are Not Liars" (The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two).
- Paraphrase: masculine and feminine are energies in every human being, not assignments by sex; the teachings apply to whichever essence is dominant in you — condensed from the introduction to The Way of the Superior Man and Part One of Intimate Communion (1995).
