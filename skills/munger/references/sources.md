# Sources — Charlie Munger

Complete bibliography for this seat, primary sources first. Dates given per item; "Talk N" numbering follows the expanded third edition of Poor Charlie's Almanack (2005).

## Primary — books and collected talks

- **Poor Charlie's Almanack: The Wit and Wisdom of Charles T. Munger**, ed. Peter D. Kaufman. Donning, 1st ed. 2005; expanded 3rd ed. 2008; abridged reissue as *Poor Charlie's Almanack: The Essential Wit and Wisdom of Charles T. Munger*, Stripe Press, 2023. The central document of the corpus: eleven talks, the "Mungerisms" compilations, and the editorial "An Investing Principles Checklist."
- **"The Psychology of Human Misjudgment."** Delivered in versions between 1992 and 1995 — including at Harvard Law School (1995, the widely circulated audio) — then substantially revised and expanded by Munger in 2005 as Talk Eleven of Poor Charlie's Almanack, enumerating the 25 standard causes of human misjudgment.

## Primary — speeches (chronological)

- **Harvard School (now Harvard-Westlake) commencement address**, Los Angeles, June 13, 1986. The inversion speech: prescriptions for guaranteed misery. Talk One in Poor Charlie's Almanack.
- **"A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom As It Relates to Investment Management and Business,"** USC Marshall School of Business, April 14, 1994. The latticework, 80-90 models, scale economics, the surfing model, circle of competence. Talk Two.
- **"A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom, Revisited,"** Stanford Law School, April 19, 1996. Talk Three.
- **"Practical Thought About Practical Thought?"** informal talk, July 20, 1996. The five helpful notions and the Coca-Cola construction exercise. Talk Four.
- **"The Need for More Multidisciplinary Skills from Professionals: Educational Implications,"** Harvard Law School 50th Reunion, April 24, 1998. Talk Five.
- **"Investment Practices of Leading Charitable Foundations,"** Foundation Financial Officers Group, Santa Monica, October 14, 1998. Costs and croupiers. Talk Six.
- **Breakfast Meeting of the Philanthropy Round Table**, Pasadena, November 10, 2000. Febezzlement and the Galbraith "bezzle." Talk Seven.
- **"The Great Financial Scandal of 2003,"** satirical essay written in 2000. Accounting-driven scandal foreseen. Talk Eight.
- **"Academic Economics: Strengths and Faults After Considering Interdisciplinary Needs,"** Herb Kay Undergraduate Lecture, UC Santa Barbara, October 3, 2003. Talk Nine.
- **USC Gould School of Law commencement address**, May 13, 2007. Deserved trust, lifelong learning, ideology warning, self-pity, assiduity. Talk Ten in later editions; full transcripts circulate independently.

## Primary — meetings and Q&A

- **Berkshire Hathaway annual meeting remarks**, 1978-2023 (vice chairman). Sources for the EBITDA remark (2003), opportunity-cost filter, macro-forecasting refusals, moat discussions, and the liquor/ladies/leverage line (Buffett crediting Munger). Transcripts and recordings via CNBC's Warren Buffett Archive.
- **Wesco Financial annual meeting remarks**, 1984-2011 (chairman). Sit-on-your-hands discipline, "not supposed to be easy." Widely circulated shareholder notes (e.g., Whitney Tilson's meeting notes).
- **Daily Journal Corporation annual meeting Q&A**, 2014-2023 (chairman through 2022). Sources for the envy line (Feb 15, 2023), fish-where-the-fish-are (2017), three-position family portfolio (2017-2021), Robinhood/"wretched excess" remarks (2021), and crypto condemnations (2022-2023). Full transcripts at latticeworkinvesting.com, Steady Compounding, and Kingswell.

## Primary — interviews and essays

- **BBC interview**, 2009. The 50%-decline equanimity standard.
- **Jason Zweig interview, The Wall Street Journal**, September 2014 ("Charlie Munger: Secrets of Buffett's Success?"). Patience, temperament, and the Berkshire method late in life.
- **Caltech Distinguished Alumnus conversation** (Zoom, December 14, 2020). The too-hard pile, rationality as life dedication, value investing's harder future.
- **"Why America Should Ban Crypto,"** Wall Street Journal op-ed, February 1, 2023. The gambling-contract characterization.
- **Acquired podcast interview** (recorded at Munger's home, released October 2023). His only dedicated long-form podcast: Costco, See's, the shrinking of easy opportunities.
- **Final CNBC interview with Becky Quick**, November 14, 2023; aired as "Charlie Munger: A Life of Wit and Wisdom," November 30, 2023, with published full transcript. "Avoid crazy at all costs"; avoiding the standard ways of failing.

## Secondary syntheses (context and cross-checking only)

- Janet Lowe, **Damn Right! Behind the Scenes with Berkshire Hathaway Billionaire Charlie Munger**. Wiley, 2000. Biography; early career, Wheeler-Munger partnership record.
- Tren Griffin, **Charlie Munger: The Complete Investor**. Columbia Business School Publishing, 2015. Organized restatement of the models and checklists.
- Warren Buffett, **Berkshire Hathaway shareholder letters** (esp. 1989 and 2014 letters crediting Munger's quality-over-price influence and the See's Candies lesson).
- **CNBC Warren Buffett Archive** (buffett.cnbc.com). Searchable video/transcripts of Berkshire meetings, used to verify meeting-sourced quotations.
