# Quotes — Charlie Munger

Short attributed excerpts only. Wording checked against transcripts and published editions where available; entries whose exact phrasing varies across transcripts are explicitly marked **paraphrase-stable** (the idea is verbatim-solid, the wording circulates in minor variants). Use sparingly, always with attribution.

## Verified wording

- "It's such a simple idea. It's the golden rule, so to speak: you want to deliver to the world what you would buy if you were on the other end." — and, immediately prior: "The safest way to get what you want is to try and deserve what you want." (USC Gould School of Law commencement, May 13, 2007; transcript.)
- "The highest form which civilization can reach is a seamless web of deserved trust." (USC Gould commencement, 2007; transcript. Minor variants: "the highest form that civilization can reach.")
- "To the man with only a hammer, every problem looks like a nail." ("A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom," USC Marshall, 1994; Munger presenting the proverb as folk wisdom he endorses.)
- "80 or 90 important models will carry about 90% of the freight in making you a worldly-wise person." (USC Marshall, 1994; transcript.)
- "Never, ever, think about something else when you should be thinking about the power of incentives." ("The Psychology of Human Misjudgment," Talk Eleven, Poor Charlie's Almanack, 2005.)
- "Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome." ("The Psychology of Human Misjudgment," 1995 talk; circulates with "I'll" and "incentives" variants.)
- "The big money is not in the buying and the selling, but in the waiting." (Poor Charlie's Almanack, 2005.)
- "I think that, every time you see the word EBITDA, you should substitute the words 'bullshit earnings.'" (Berkshire Hathaway annual meeting, 2003; transcript variants exist — e.g., "you just substituted the phrase.")
- "The world is not driven by greed. It's driven by envy." (Daily Journal annual meeting, February 15, 2023; transcript.)
- "Avoid crazy at all costs. Crazy is way more common than you think. It's easy to slip into crazy. Just avoid it, avoid it, avoid it." (Final CNBC interview with Becky Quick, November 14, 2023; aired in "Charlie Munger: A Life of Wit and Wisdom," November 30, 2023.)
- "I just try to avoid being stupid. I have a way of handling a lot of problems — I put them in what I call my 'too hard pile,' and just leave them there." (Caltech Distinguished Alumnus conversation, December 2020; transcript.)
- "My life has been dedicated rather intensely to rationality." (Caltech Distinguished Alumnus conversation, December 2020.)
- "A cryptocurrency is not a currency, not a commodity, and not a security. Instead, it's a gambling contract with a nearly 100% edge for the house." ("Why America Should Ban Crypto," Wall Street Journal op-ed, February 1, 2023.)
- "It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent." (Poor Charlie's Almanack; also cited in Berkshire meeting compilations.)
- "What a man wishes, that also will he believe." (Demosthenes, quoted by Munger in Talk Eleven, Poor Charlie's Almanack, as the epigraph for overoptimism.)

## Paraphrase-stable (idea verbatim, wording varies by transcript)

- "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there." (Attributed across Berkshire meeting lore and Poor Charlie's Almanack; the corpus's one-line statement of inversion.)
- "Invert, always invert." (Munger's rendering of Jacobi's "man muss immer umkehren"; cited in Poor Charlie's Almanack and multiple talks.)
- "Knowing what you don't know is more useful than being brilliant." (Widely attributed; consistent with Caltech 2020 and Poor Charlie's Almanack statements on the circle of competence.)
- "Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up." (Poor Charlie's Almanack; the USC Gould 2007 address carries the same prescription in longer form.)
- "A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price." (Poor Charlie's Almanack; the underlying argument is verbatim in the 1994 USC talk's return-on-capital passage.)
- "Take a simple idea and take it seriously." (Attributed across Poor Charlie's Almanack and meeting transcripts; sometimes rendered "take a simple idea and take it very seriously.")
- "It's not supposed to be easy. Anyone who finds it easy is stupid." (Wesco/Berkshire meeting remarks; multiple transcript variants.)
- "The human mind is a lot like the human egg: when one sperm gets in, it shuts down so the next one can't." (Poor Charlie's Almanack; wording varies across renditions of the first-conclusion image.)
- "Micro-economics is what we do; macro is what we have to put up with." (Berkshire meeting remarks; transcript variants.)
- "There are only three ways a smart person can go broke: liquor, ladies, and leverage." (Buffett quoting Munger at Berkshire meetings; secondhand within the corpus, flag as such when used.)
- "Envy is a really stupid sin because it's the only one you could never possibly have any fun at." (Berkshire meeting remarks; multiple compilations, minor variants.)
- "The two rules of fishing: fish where the fish are, and don't forget rule number one." (Daily Journal Q&A, 2017; wording varies slightly by transcript.)
- "If you're not willing to react with equanimity to a market price decline of 50% two or three times a century, you deserve the mediocre result you're going to get." (BBC interview, 2009; commonly quoted with small variations.)
