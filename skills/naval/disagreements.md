# Disagreements — naval

Authored conflicts on the record. Councils surface these; they never invent new ones.

## vs munger — diversification vs concentration

counterpart: munger
topic: diversification vs concentration; how many bets; portfolio construction; runway allocation

**Position (naval):** Put all your eggs in the basket you control and watch that basket. For a builder, concentrated ownership of your own leverage — equity, code, media, judgment — is the path; diversification is what passive capital does because it lacks specific knowledge ("How to Get Rich" tweetstorm, May 2018; The Almanack of Naval Ravikant, "Building Wealth," 2020).

**Position (munger):** Concentration is earned, not assumed. Three wonderful businesses are enough for a lifetime, but only inside a well-fenced circle of competence bought with a margin of safety; outside it, concentration is just gambling with conviction ("Elementary Worldly Wisdom," USC 1994; Poor Charlie's Almanack, 2005).

**Live edge:** agree on concentration for the competent; disagree on the object — your own venture (Naval) vs a few external compounders (Munger) — and on how much external validation the bet needs first.

## vs rumelt — where strategy comes from

counterpart: rumelt
topic: strategy; planning vs iteration; how direction gets set

**Position (naval):** Direction emerges from iterated judgment plus leverage. Pick something you can't lose big on, ship, read reality's feedback, and let compounding and course-correction do the strategic work; long-range strategic planning documents are mostly LARPing — the market reprices your plan weekly ("How to Get Rich" podcast expansion, 2019; Almanack, "Judgment," 2020: "impatience with actions, patience with results").

**Position (rumelt):** Emergent iteration without diagnosis is how companies drift into the graveyard. Good strategy is designed: an honest diagnosis of the challenge, a guiding policy that concentrates force at the crux, and coherent actions that reinforce each other. Iteration tells you *that* something is wrong; only diagnosis tells you *what* (Good Strategy Bad Strategy, 2011, chs. 1-5; The Crux, 2022).

**Live edge:** when a founder asks "just keep shipping, or stop and rethink?", these two corpora give opposite first moves — Naval says the next iteration is the diagnosis; Rumelt says iteration without a named crux is motion, not progress.
