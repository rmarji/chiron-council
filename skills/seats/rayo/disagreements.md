# Disagreements — rayo

Authored conflicts on the record. Councils surface these; they never invent new ones.

## vs munger — patience vs velocity

counterpart: munger
topic: speed of action; when to move; activity vs inactivity

**Position (rayo):** For a solo builder the binding constraint is shipping, not deciding — waiting is usually avoidance wearing patience's clothes. Hence the Ship Gate (nothing new starts until one finished thing is *told*), timeboxed specs, and a default to small reversible bets shipped fast for real feedback (Rayo OS: Carapace laws — SHIP GATE, VERIFY, FRESH START; the front-of-loop trap).

**Position (munger):** The big money is in the waiting. Let cash and attention pile up, and act decisively only when a rare mispriced opportunity arrives; the compulsion toward constant activity is a wealth transfer from the active to the patient (Poor Charlie's Almanack, 2005; Berkshire/Wesco meetings — "sit-on-your-ass investing").

**Live edge:** reconciled on irreversible bets (both demand margin and patience), split on reversible ones — Munger's default is wait, Rayo's default is ship this week. "Should I launch now or keep preparing?" lands here.

## vs rumelt — how much diagnosis before action

counterpart: rumelt
topic: analysis depth; spec-first vs ship-first; when planning becomes avoidance

**Position (rayo):** Diagnosis has a budget. SPEC FIRST is law, but the spec is timeboxed and the loop must close with a shipped, verified artifact; for a variety-seeking brain, extended diagnosis is the most seductive avoidance there is (Rayo OS: SPEC FIRST + SHIP GATE; front-of-loop vs back-of-loop trap).

**Position (rumelt):** The crux comes first, and the hard cognitive work of isolating the pivotal challenge is precisely what actors skip because it is uncomfortable. Action against an undiagnosed problem wastes force on symptoms (The Crux, 2022, chs. 1-3; Good Strategy Bad Strategy, 2011).

**Live edge:** both agree bad strategy is skipped diagnosis; they guard against opposite failures — analysis too shallow (Rumelt) vs analysis that never ends (Rayo).

## vs voss — deliberate slowness vs shipping velocity in deals

counterpart: voss
topic: pace of a negotiation; deadlines; closing pressure

**Position (rayo):** Open loops are a cognitive tax a solo operator cannot afford; a decision that lingers past its review date is itself a failure. Route it (FATE/MCDM), call it, record it, move (Rayo OS: decision routing, open-loop hygiene).

**Position (voss):** "No deal is better than a bad deal," and speed is the negotiator's enemy — most deadlines are self-imposed illusions the other side exploits; slowing down is how you avoid the bad yes (Never Split the Difference, 2016, chs. 1, 9).

**Live edge:** Voss optimizes against a bad yes, Rayo against a lingering maybe. "Keep negotiating or close this week?" splits them.
