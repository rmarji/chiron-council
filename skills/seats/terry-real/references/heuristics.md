# Heuristics — the Terry Real corpus

The full set of operational rules, in IF/THEN form, each attributed. These are decision rules the corpus applies, not slogans.

## Catching the whoosh

- **IF you feel the whoosh** (flooding, surge of rage or collapse) **THEN do nothing yet** — no speaking, no texting, no deciding. The adaptive child is driving and every skill is offline until the wise adult returns (*Us*, 2022).
- **IF you cannot tell whether you are regressed THEN ask "how old do I feel right now?"** An answer under about twenty-five means wait (*Us*, 2022; RLT training materials).
- **IF you are too flooded to stay respectful THEN call a responsible time-out on yourself** — "I'm getting reactive, I need twenty minutes" — with a named return time, and actually return (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007).
- **IF the urge is to build your case during the break THEN stop** — case-building is the adaptive child rehearsing losing strategy #1, not regulation (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007; *Us*, 2022).

## Choosing the move

- **IF you are winning the argument on the facts THEN check what it is costing** — being right and being close are usually a trade; pick closeness ("you can be right or you can be married," *Fierce Intimacy*, 2018).
- **IF what you are about to say is a complaint about the past THEN convert it into a request about the future** — say what you want, not what was wrong (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007, winning strategy one).
- **IF you must raise a grievance THEN run the feedback wheel:** what you saw or heard, what you made up about it, how you feel, what you would like — one event, four sentences, then let go of the outcome (Janet Hurley's wheel via *The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007).
- **IF you are about to say "but" THEN replace it with "and"** — "but" cancels everything before it (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007).
- **IF "honesty" would demean your partner THEN it is not honesty, it is unbridled self-expression** — feel everything, say what serves the relationship, with full respect (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007; *Fierce Intimacy*, 2018).
- **IF you want your partner to give you something THEN ask what you can give to help them give it** — empowerment beats control every time (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007, winning strategy four).

## Stance checks

- **IF you notice contempt, superiority, or the urge to instruct THEN you are one-up: come down to "same as" before speaking** (*Us*, 2022; *I Don't Want to Talk About It*, 1997).
- **IF you notice groveling, self-erasure, or eggshell-walking THEN you are one-down: come up to "same as" — accountability never requires collapse** (*I Don't Want to Talk About It*, 1997; *Us*, 2022).
- **IF you feel wronged and therefore entitled to be cruel THEN stop — that is offending from the victim position**, the signature form of retaliation (*Fierce Intimacy*, 2018; *Us*, 2022).
- **IF you are keeping score THEN the ledger itself is the problem** — there are no individual winners inside an "us"; a win over your partner is a loss for the ecosystem you live in (*Us*, 2022).
- **IF anger is present THEN keep the anger and drop the contempt** — direct, firm, and respectful can coexist; angry and demeaning cannot (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007, full-respect living).

## Repair

- **IF the relationship is stuck in disharmony THEN someone must move first — let it be you.** Waiting for the partner to soften first is a stalemate strategy, and the one who reaches for repair is not the one who lost (*Us*, 2022).
- **IF you are entering a repair conversation THEN remember love before you open your mouth** — reconnect with the part of you that wants this person, or the "repair" will be round two (*Us*, 2022).
- **IF your partner offers a repair attempt, however clumsy, THEN receive it with generosity** — take what you can, respond to the reach, not the wording (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007, winning strategy three; *Us*, 2022: "you have to help your partner come through for you").
- **IF you owe an acknowledgment THEN own your specific part without a global confession** — clean accountability, no self-flagellation, no "sorry you feel that way" (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007).
- **IF the same scripted fight keeps recurring THEN install a dead-stop contract:** agree that when either partner flags their core negative image being triggered, the other comes to a dead stop whether or not they agree with the perception (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007).
- **IF hatred for your partner surfaces in a long relationship THEN normalize before you catastrophize** — normal marital hatred visits everyone; "everyone gets to go crazy in long-term relationships, but you have to take turns" (*Us*, 2022).

## Scope and safety

- **IF there is abuse, violence, intimidation, or coercive control THEN the repair frame is suspended** — nothing short of physical safety overrides every contract; safety first, professionals first (*The New Rules of Marriage*, 2007).
- **IF a man "isn't depressed" but drinks, rages, or vanishes into work THEN look under the defense for covert depression** — treat the pain, not just the behavior (*I Don't Want to Talk About It*, 1997).
- **IF the inherited move is pulling hard THEN the moment of resisting it is the whole game** — change is an accumulation of these acts of relational heroism, not an insight (*I Don't Want to Talk About It*, 1997).
