# Heuristics — Naval Ravikant

The full set of operational rules in the corpus, in IF/THEN form, each attributed. These are decision rules, not slogans: each one should change what the user does next.

## Wealth and career

- **IF income only rises when hours rise, THEN stop optimizing the rate and build an asset** — the ceiling is the hours-for-dollars structure itself; own equity or create a product that sells without you ("You're not going to get rich renting out your time" — tweetstorm, May 2018).
- **IF choosing what to work on, THEN follow the obsession that feels like play to you and looks like work to others** — that asymmetry marks specific knowledge, the edge that can't be trained into a replacement (tweetstorm, 2018; "Arm Yourself With Specific Knowledge," How to Get Rich podcast, 2019).
- **IF a path requires a gatekeeper's permission (funding, platform, promotion), THEN check for a permissionless route first** — code and media need nobody's yes and replicate at zero marginal cost (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF you can either sell or build but not both, THEN learn the other one** — "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable" (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF offered influence without accountability, THEN decline and take the version with your name on it** — society pays equity, responsibility, and leverage only to those who take public risk (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF a task can be outsourced for less than your aspirational hourly rate, THEN outsource, automate, or delete it** — set the rate absurdly high and enforce it, including on meetings and "coffee" (tweetstorm, 2018; "Set an Aspirational Hourly Rate," How to Get Rich podcast, 2019).
- **IF deciding how hard to push, THEN fix who-and-what before how-hard** — "Work as hard as you can. Even though who you work with and what you work on are more important than how hard you work" (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF the market position is crowded, THEN differentiate by authenticity rather than out-competing** — nobody can compete with you on being you; the internet scales any genuine niche (How to Get Rich podcast, "Escape Competition Through Authenticity," 2019).

## Partners and games

- **IF evaluating a partner, THEN screen for intelligence, energy, and above all integrity — and judge integrity by actions, not words** (tweetstorm, 2018; How to Get Rich podcast, 2019).
- **IF a prospective partner is a cynic or a pessimist, THEN walk away regardless of talent** — "their beliefs are self-fulfilling" (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF a move wins this round by burning trust, THEN refuse it, even at a profit** — all returns come from compound interest, and extraction ends the iteration (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF an industry or scene doesn't allow decade-long relationships, THEN pick a different industry** — "Pick an industry where you can play long term games with long term people" (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF a rare compounding person or project appears, THEN concentrate on it and drop the rest** — "99% of effort is wasted"; the discipline is going all-in on the 1% that compounds (Almanack, "Building Judgment").

## Decisions and judgment

- **IF you cannot decide after genuine deliberation, THEN the answer is no** — under modern option abundance, a maybe is a no (Almanack, "Building Judgment").
- **IF the decision is reversible, THEN decide fast; IF irreversible, THEN slow down and gather** (Almanack, "Building Judgment").
- **IF evenly split on a difficult decision, THEN take the path more painful in the short term** — the mind overweights near-term pain, so the conflict itself is the signal (Almanack, "Building Judgment").
- **IF any bet can take you out of the game entirely — jail, ruin, reputation death — THEN refuse it regardless of expected value** (Kelly criterion framing; "Kelly Criterion: Avoid Ruin," How to Get Rich podcast, 2019).
- **IF you notice yourself performing intelligence rather than seeking truth, THEN drop the performance** — status motives corrupt judgment, and judgment is the paid skill (Almanack, "Building Judgment"; Knowledge Project #18, 2017).
- **IF progress feels slow, THEN be impatient with actions and patient with results** — act now, but give compounding its years (Almanack, "Building Judgment").

## Luck and visibility

- **IF working in stealth, THEN ship and publish** — luck surface area = doing x telling; untold work produces no fourth-kind luck ("Making Money Isn't About Luck," How to Get Rich podcast, 2019; Luck = Doing x Telling after Jason Roberts).
- **IF waiting for opportunity, THEN become known for one specific, verifiable strength** — the fourth kind of luck makes opportunity seek you, pre-filtered (How to Get Rich podcast, 2019).

## Learning

- **IF building a reading habit, THEN read what you love until you love to read — quality curation comes after the habit exists** (tweetstorm, 2018).
- **IF choosing what to study, THEN take foundations over fashions** — math, logic, microeconomics, evolution, persuasion; read the originals and re-read the great books rather than skimming trends (tweetstorm, 2018; Knowledge Project #18, 2017).
- **IF torn between consuming and doing, THEN do** — "Doing is faster than watching" (tweetstorm, 2018, near-verbatim).

## Inner game

- **IF you catch a new desire forming, THEN price it before signing** — every desire is "a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want"; hold one big desire at a time, consciously (Almanack, "Learning Happiness"; Knowledge Project #18, 2017).
- **IF unhappy despite success, THEN subtract rather than add** — happiness is what remains when the sense of something missing is removed; audit desires, inputs, and status games before acquiring anything new (Almanack, "Learning Happiness").
- **IF comparing yourself to someone enviable, THEN take the whole-life test** — you cannot cherry-pick their outcomes; you'd have to swap entire lives, problems included. If you wouldn't, the envy is incoherent (Almanack, "Learning Happiness").
- **IF sacrificing every today for an imaginary tomorrow, THEN restructure toward work that is complete in itself** — that is the corpus's actual definition of retirement (Almanack; Joe Rogan Experience #1309, 2019).
- **IF optimizing wealth while health or peace degrades, THEN reorder** — the terminal goods are "a fit body, a calm mind, a house full of love," and they must be earned, not bought (Almanack, epigraph; tweet, 2018).
