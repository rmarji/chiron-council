# Mental Models — David Deida

Every named model or concept the corpus uses, with origin and when it applies. Register: third-person analytic; the corpus is prescriptive spiritual teaching, not clinical psychology.

## Sexual essence (orientation, not gender)

Origin: Intimate Communion (1995), Part One; restated in The Way of the Superior Man (1997), introduction. Every person carries both masculine and feminine energy, but most have a dominant "sexual essence" — the pole they most deeply identify with in intimacy — which is independent of biological sex and of sexual orientation. Deida is explicit that some men have a feminine essence, some women a masculine one, and that the teachings apply by essence, not anatomy; the 2017 anniversary materials for The Way of the Superior Man repeat this clarification. When it applies: any time the model risks being read as gender prescription — the essence concept is the corpus's own answer to that misreading, and it should be kept visible.

## Sexual polarity

Origin: Intimate Communion (1995); The Way of the Superior Man, Part Three ("Working with Polarity and Energy"). Attraction operates like magnetism: charge flows across the difference between masculine and feminine poles. Neutralize the poles — both partners guarding, negotiating, managing — and the charge drops to zero regardless of affection. Polarity can be consciously animated: partners who are equals in daily logistics can still choose polarized intimate time. When it applies: diagnosing flat attraction, "roommate" dynamics, and the trade-offs of egalitarian sameness.

## The three stages of relationship

Origin: Intimate Communion (1995), where the stages give the book its title; echoed in It's a Guy Thing (1997) and workshop teaching. Presented honestly:

- **Stage 1 — Dependence.** Traditional, rigidly-roled relationship (breadwinner/homemaker, macho/submissive). High polarity, high structure, security through fixed roles — at the cost of freedom, growth, and often fairness. Deida does not romanticize it; stage 1 masculine is the "macho" mode he treats as underdeveloped.
- **Stage 2 — Independence (the 50/50 relationship).** Two autonomous equals who negotiate everything and keep safe boundaries. An ethical advance over stage 1 — Deida calls the sensitive, egalitarian stage-2 man an improvement — but polarity typically collapses, producing respectful, de-sexualized partnership. Stage 2's failure mode is mistaking safety for depth.
- **Stage 3 — Intimate communion.** Equality of worth and full polarity held simultaneously: both partners are whole, neither depends on the other for identity, and each consciously gives their gift — one animating consciousness/direction, the other radiance/energy — as practice, by choice. Direction of travel is always toward stage 3, never regression to stage 1.

When it applies: locating a couple's dynamic before prescribing anything; distinguishing "go back to traditional roles" (which the corpus rejects) from "go forward through equality into conscious polarity" (which it teaches).

## The edge

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, Part One, "Know Your Real Edge and Don't Fake It" and "Lean Just Beyond Your Edge." A person's edge is the honest boundary of their current capacity and courage. Three positions relative to it: hiding short of it (comfort, stagnation), faking beyond it (bravado, collapse under pressure), and leaning just beyond it — the optimal growth dose: "In any given moment, a man's growth is optimized if he leans just beyond his edge, his capacity, his fear." When it applies: career risk, hard conversations, creative work, any decision where fear is the operative variable.

## Purpose-first architecture

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, Part One, "Your Purpose Must Come Before Your Relationship" and "If You Don't Know Your Purpose, Discover It, Now." The masculine core organizes around mission; relationship is the second priority, not because love matters less but because a person without direction brings neediness instead of fullness to love. Purpose is layered — specific projects expire, and beneath each lies a deeper purpose to be discovered ("Be Willing to Change Everything in Your Life," Part One). When it applies: someone orbiting their partner, deferring their direction for relationship comfort, or panicking when a career chapter ends.

## Masculine as consciousness, feminine as energy

Origin: Blue Truth (2002/2005); Dear Lover (2005); Finding God Through Sex (2002/2005). The corpus's metaphysical pairing: the masculine pole is witness, stillness, direction, emptiness; the feminine pole is light, movement, emotion, fullness — and every person contains both. In intimacy they are complementary gifts: consciousness offers steadiness and depth; energy offers aliveness and radiance. Held as energies, the pairing describes a dance; held as gender law, it misleads — the corpus's own essence teaching (above) is the corrective. When it applies: explaining why presence (not advice) answers emotion, and why radiance (not argument) answers absence.

## Her storm is a test of presence

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, Part Four, "Her Complaint Is Content-Free" and "What She Wants Is Not What She Says"; Part Two, "Stay with Her Intensity—To a Point." A partner's mood, resistance, or provocation is modeled as an implicit test: not manipulation but an embodied question — is your presence stable, or will you collapse, flee, or turn cold? Passing means staying open, humorous, and loving under pressure; failing means reactivity or withdrawal. The corpus adds its own limit: intensity that crosses into abuse is not a test to endure ("Stay with Her Intensity—To a Point"). When it applies: recurring fights where the literal topic keeps changing but the pattern repeats.

## Women are not liars (truth of the moment)

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two, "Women Are Not Liars." Feminine speech in emotional moments expresses the truth of the moment, not sworn testimony about facts or the future; "I never want to see you again," said in a storm, reports the storm. Holding a feminine-essence partner to emotional statements as if they were contracts is a category error. When it applies: decoding apparent inconsistency; defusing arguments about "but you said."

## The gift

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, Part One, "Do It for Love" and "Enjoy Your Friends' Criticism"; Blue Truth (2002/2005). Each person has a deepest gift — the particular love, art, or direction only they can give — and the core error is withholding it while waiting for safety, certainty, or permission. Deida's practices (edge work, purpose discovery, men's counsel who "enjoy your friends' criticism") all serve gift-delivery. When it applies: procrastinated callings, permission-seeking, playing small in work or love.

## Fear never fully resolves

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, "Lean Just Beyond Your Edge"; Blue Truth (2002/2005). Fear is a permanent companion at the edge, not a signal to wait. Courage is redefined as acting at the edge while afraid; comfort is redefined as a warning sign of stagnation. When it applies: any "I'll do it when I feel ready" construction.

## Incompletion (stop hoping to be done)

Origin: The Way of the Superior Man, Part One, "Stop Hoping for a Completion of Anything in Life." Life's tasks never finish — inbox, mission, relationship maintenance are perpetual — so postponing love, presence, or purpose until things settle is structural self-deception. Peace is found in open-hearted engagement amid incompletion, not after it. When it applies: "once this project ships / once things calm down" reasoning.

## Intimacy as yoga

Origin: Finding God Through Sex (2002/2005); Enlightened Sex audio program (Sounds True, 2004); Wild Nights (2000/2005). Sexuality and relationship are treated as spiritual practice — repeatable disciplines of breath, attention, and openness in which pleasure and conflict alike train the capacity to stay open. This is the corpus's frame for why one practices presence at all: not to manage a partner, but to grow consciousness and love. When it applies: reframing relationship difficulty from "problem to escape" to "practice ground chosen on purpose."
