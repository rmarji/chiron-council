# Quotes — Alex Hormozi

Verified short excerpts with attribution. Verbatim wording was checked against the published books' quoted text (Goodreads author-quote pages for $100M Offers and $100M Leads) and the book titles/subtitles themselves. ## From $100M Offers (2021)

- "$100M Offers: How To Make Offers So Good People Feel Stupid Saying No." (book title/subtitle — the central thesis of the offer work.)
- "The business that provides the most value wins. Period." (Hormozi's $100M corpus; aggregators split on $100M Offers vs $100M Leads, so cite the corpus rather than a single book.)
- "Make an offer so good people feel stupid saying no." **(near-verbatim; the book's own compression of its subtitle; appears in this and close variants throughout.)**
- "We are the result of our actions, not our aspirations." (Alex Hormozi, public posts / The Game corpus, 2026; not from the books.)

### Frameworks stated in the book (paraphrase where wording varies)

- The Value Equation: "Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)." (ch.6 "The Value Equation" — the equation as printed; the surrounding directive is to make offers "immediate, seamless, and effortless.") **(equation verbatim; directive near-verbatim.)**
- "Reversing risk is the number one way to increase the conversion of an offer." (ch.15 "Guarantees.") **(near-verbatim; the guarantee chapter's thesis.)**
- On bonuses vs discounts: a single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into components and stacked; a bonus adds value at constant price where a discount subtracts it. **(paraphrase of ch.14 "Bonuses.")**

## From $100M Leads (2023)

- "$100M Leads: How to Get Strangers To Want To Buy Your Stuff." (book title/subtitle.)
- "A lead is a person you can contact." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads, Section II.)
- "We want engaged leads: people who show interest in the stuff you sell." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads.)
- "The leads showing interest are the leads that matter." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads.)
- "People don't have shorter attention spans, they have higher standards." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads.)
- "A person who pays with their time now is more likely to pay with their money later." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads — the lead-magnet rationale.)
- "You get more customers by getting: More Leads. Better Leads. Cheaper Leads. Reliably." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads.) **(punctuation reconstructed from the list form.)**
- "The more ways you measure, the more ways you can win." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads.)
- "Cold outreach is a numbers game. The more people you reach out to, the more engaged leads you get." (verified quote attributed to $100M Leads, "Cold Outreach.")

### Lead-generation definitions (paraphrase)

- A lead magnet is "something you give away for free in exchange for someone's contact information," and it should be so valuable people feel they should have paid for it. **(near-verbatim first clause; second clause paraphrased from Section III "Lead Magnets.")**
- The Core Four are the only ways to let strangers know about your stuff: warm outreach, post free content, cold outreach, run paid ads. **(paraphrase of Section III "Get Leads.")**
