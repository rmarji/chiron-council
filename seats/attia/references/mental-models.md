# Mental models — the Peter Attia corpus

Every named model or concept the published corpus uses, defined, with origin and when it applies. Sources are *Outlive* (Peter Attia with Bill Gifford, Harmony, 2023) by chapter, named *Drive* podcast episodes, and peterattiamd.com articles. Study aid; not medical advice.

## Healthspan vs. lifespan

*Definition:* Lifespan is how long you live; healthspan is how long you live in good physical and cognitive health, free of chronic disease and disability. Longevity is the balance of both (*Outlive*, ch. 1). *When it applies:* Any time a health decision trades quantity of years against quality — the corpus insists both be on the ledger.

## Medicine 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0

*Definition:* A three-era model of medicine. 1.0 is pre-scientific. 2.0 is the current germ-theory, evidence-based paradigm that beat acute and infectious disease but treats chronic disease reactively. 3.0 is the corpus's proposed shift: prevention over treatment, personalization over trial averages, explicit risk calculus, and equal weight on healthspan (*Outlive*, ch. 2-3). *When it applies:* Framing why "my numbers are normal" is not the same as "I am optimizing for a long healthspan."

## The Four Horsemen

*Definition:* The four chronic-disease categories that cause most deaths in non-smokers over 50 — (1) atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, (2) cancer, (3) neurodegenerative disease (chiefly Alzheimer's), and (4) metabolic dysfunction / type 2 diabetes (*Outlive*, ch. 3). *When it applies:* Prioritizing what to actually prevent; the audit of "what is most likely to end my healthspan."

## The Centenarian Decathlon

*Definition:* A personalized set of the ~10 physical tasks you want to be able to perform in your final decades — some athletic (hike, carry a pack), some daily-living (get off the floor, lift a suitcase overhead), some personal (paddle, play with grandkids). It functions as training "scaffolding" (*Outlive*, ch. 11; Drive #261, 2023). *When it applies:* Setting concrete, meaningful fitness targets instead of vague "stay healthy" goals.

## The Marginal Decade

*Definition:* The final decade of life, typically marked by physical and cognitive decline. The corpus argues this decline is not purely genetic destiny but largely the result of decades of under-preparation (*Outlive*, ch. 3, 11; Drive #261, 2023). *When it applies:* Motivating why training targets today must be set high — the Marginal Decade is where capacity runs out first.

## Back-casting

*Definition:* Working backward from the capacities you want in your Marginal Decade to set today's training targets. Because strength, VO2 max, and stability all decline with age, the corpus argues you must build a large surplus now to still have enough later; the Decathlon "allows us to backcast from there" (*Outlive*, ch. 11). *When it applies:* Designing a training plan whose intensity is justified by the far-future goal, not present comfort.

## The four pillars of exercise

*Definition:* The corpus's four training domains — (1) **stability** (the foundational ability to control the body and avoid injury), (2) **strength** (muscle and force production), (3) **zone 2** aerobic base (the highest intensity sustainable while keeping blood lactate below ~2 mmol/L, i.e. still fat-burning and conversational), and (4) **VO2 max / zone 5** anaerobic capacity (maximal aerobic power) (*Outlive*, ch. 11-12; Drive #261, 2023). *When it applies:* Structuring a complete longevity training program rather than doing only one modality.

## Zone 2

*Definition:* A training intensity — the highest steady-state effort you can hold while keeping lactate under about 2 mmol/L, roughly the pace at which you can still hold a conversation. It builds mitochondrial density and metabolic flexibility (*Outlive*, ch. 12; Drive #261, 2023). *When it applies:* Building the aerobic and metabolic base; the corpus recommends multiple hours per week.

## VO2 max

*Definition:* The maximum rate of oxygen the body can use during intense exercise; the corpus's single best modifiable marker of cardiorespiratory fitness and, it argues, of mortality risk (Drive #217, 2022; AMA #27, 2021). *When it applies:* Judging overall fitness and its enormous mortality signal; the corpus targets high age-adjusted VO2 max deliberately.

## The five tactical domains

*Definition:* The five levers of Medicine 3.0 — exercise, nutrition, sleep, emotional health, and exogenous molecules (drugs, hormones, supplements) (*Outlive*, ch. 3, 11-17). *When it applies:* Building a complete plan; the corpus orders them with exercise first and emotional health foundational, molecules last.

## Nutritional biochemistry

*Definition:* The corpus's replacement for "diet" — treating food as inputs to individual metabolism, judged by the person's own biomarkers (glucose via CGM, lipids, body composition) rather than by diet labels or ideology (*Outlive*, ch. 15). *When it applies:* Any nutrition decision; the model rejects one-size-fits-all diets in favor of personalized data.

## CR / DR / TR (the three dietary levers)

*Definition:* The corpus organizes nutrition tactics into three ways to manage intake — **CR** (caloric restriction: eat less overall), **DR** (dietary restriction: eliminate a category of food), and **TR** (time restriction: compress the eating window, e.g. fasting). Each is a tool, none is mandatory, and the corpus is wary of fasting's muscle-loss cost if overdone (*Outlive*, ch. 15-16). *When it applies:* Choosing an intake strategy that fits the individual's metabolic goals without dogma.

## apoB (apolipoprotein B)

*Definition:* The protein carried by every atherogenic lipoprotein particle (LDL, VLDL, IDL, Lp(a)); measuring apoB counts the number of disease-causing particles directly, which the corpus argues is a better cardiovascular risk marker than standard LDL-C. Lowering apoB early and for long is the lever against atherosclerosis (Drive #229, 2022; #334, with Tom Dayspring). *When it applies:* Assessing and acting on cardiovascular risk.

## Lp(a) (lipoprotein little-a)

*Definition:* A largely genetic, particularly atherogenic lipoprotein; the corpus recommends measuring it at least once in a lifetime because a high level meaningfully raises cardiovascular risk and changes how aggressively you manage other risk factors (Drive #229, 2022). *When it applies:* Baseline cardiovascular risk stratification.

## Prevention triad for cancer

*Definition:* The corpus's three-part cancer strategy — (1) prevent it (metabolic health, avoid smoking, limit alcohol), (2) treat it with smarter, targeted therapies, and (3) detect it as early as possible through aggressive screening, because most cancers are far more survivable when caught early (*Outlive*, ch. 8; Drive #391, 2026). *When it applies:* Reasoning about the cancer Horseman.

## Evidence-informed vs. evidence-based

*Definition:* The corpus prefers being "evidence-informed" — using trial data while asking how a specific patient resembles or differs from the average participant — over rigid "evidence-based" application of population averages to individuals (*Outlive*, ch. 3). *When it applies:* Deciding whether a study's headline result should change *this* person's plan.

## Distress tolerance / emotional health as a domain

*Definition:* The corpus elevates emotional health (managing distress, relationships, meaning) to a full tactical domain equal to the physical ones, arguing physical and emotional health are tightly linked and that longevity is meaningless without wellbeing (*Outlive*, ch. 17). *When it applies:* Whenever a plan optimizes the body while ignoring the mind.
