# Heuristics — the Peter Attia corpus

The full set of operational decision rules from the published corpus, in IF/THEN form, each attributed. Sources: *Outlive* (Peter Attia with Bill Gifford, Harmony, 2023) by chapter; named *Drive* episodes; peterattiamd.com. Study aid; not medical advice; individualized decisions belong with a physician.

## Prioritization and framing

- **IF you want the longevity intervention with the most evidence behind it, THEN train — start with exercise before anything else.** The corpus holds cardiorespiratory fitness is more strongly associated with lower mortality than any other modifiable marker (Drive #217, 2022; AMA #27, 2021).
- **IF you are choosing where to spend prevention effort, THEN audit the Four Horsemen and attack the earliest-acting, highest-leverage lever first.** Heart disease, cancer, neurodegeneration, and metabolic disease cause most deaths; each rewards early action (*Outlive*, ch. 3-9).
- **IF you want a complete plan, THEN work the five tactical domains in order: exercise, nutrition, sleep, emotional health, then exogenous molecules.** Molecules are last, not first (*Outlive*, ch. 3, 11-17).
- **IF you are relying on "normal" reference ranges to feel safe, THEN reassess against longevity targets, not population norms.** Standard ranges reflect a sick population, not optimization (Drive #229, 2022).

## Exercise

- **IF you are sedentary or low-fitness, THEN moving up even one fitness band is the single highest-return health move available.** Going from the bottom VO2 max quartile toward average roughly halves ten-year mortality risk in the data the corpus cites (AMA #27, 2021, citing Mandsager et al. 2018).
- **IF you are designing training for old age, THEN back-cast from your Marginal Decade — set today's targets from what you want to do at 85, not from present comfort.** Capacity declines with age, so build a surplus now (*Outlive*, ch. 11; Drive #261, 2023).
- **IF you do only cardio, THEN add strength and stability — muscle and control protect the Marginal Decade.** "Never has a 90-year-old wished for less muscle" (*Outlive*, ch. 11).
- **IF you are building an aerobic base, THEN accumulate multiple hours per week of zone 2 (conversational, lactate under ~2 mmol/L) and add short VO2 max intervals to raise the ceiling.** Both the base and the peak matter (*Outlive*, ch. 12; Drive #261, 2023).

## Cardiovascular / lipids

- **IF you are assessing cardiovascular risk, THEN measure apoB, not just LDL-C.** apoB counts the atherogenic particles that actually drive atherosclerosis (Drive #229, 2022).
- **IF you have never had it measured, THEN check Lp(a) at least once in your life.** It is largely genetic and, if high, changes how aggressively you manage everything else (Drive #229, 2022).
- **IF apoB is elevated, THEN the lever is to lower it early and hold it low for years — with your physician.** Plaque compounds over decades, so time-in-prevention dominates (*Outlive*, ch. 7).

## Nutrition

- **IF you are choosing what to eat, THEN anchor on protein and energy balance and personalize by your own biomarkers rather than adopting a named diet.** "Nutritional biochemistry" replaces diet dogma (*Outlive*, ch. 15-16).
- **IF you are trying to preserve muscle, especially with age, THEN eat substantially more protein than the RDA — on the order of ~1 gram per pound of body weight.** Protein defends the muscle that protects the Marginal Decade (*Outlive*, ch. 15).
- **IF you are considering fasting or aggressive time restriction, THEN watch the muscle-loss cost and don't treat it as automatically optimal.** TR is one tool among CR/DR/TR, not a mandate (*Outlive*, ch. 16).
- **IF you want to know whether a food suits you, THEN measure your response (e.g., glucose via CGM) rather than trusting the label.** Personalize by data (*Outlive*, ch. 15).

## Sleep

- **IF you are tempted to cut sleep to get more done, THEN don't — protect ~7-9 hours as non-negotiable infrastructure.** Short sleep drives metabolic, cardiovascular, and cognitive harm (Drive #47-49, 2019).
- **IF sleep quality is poor, THEN fix behavior and environment (consistency, light, temperature, caffeine/alcohol timing) before reaching for aids.** Address causes first (Drive #126, 2020; #221, 2022).

## Cancer

- **IF cancer is a concern, THEN favor early and broad screening over waiting for symptoms.** Most cancers are far more survivable caught early; the corpus screens earlier than default guidelines for informed patients (*Outlive*, ch. 8; Drive #391, 2026).

## Exogenous molecules

- **IF you are reaching for a supplement, drug, or hormone for longevity, THEN do it last and grade it by evidence — after exercise, nutrition, sleep, and emotional health.** Molecules are the fifth domain, not the first (*Outlive*, ch. 3; AMA #35, 2022).
- **IF an intervention is hyped as an anti-aging breakthrough, THEN demand human evidence and a plausible mechanism before believing it.** The corpus rates rapamycin as promising-but-unproven, metformin and fasting as uncertain, resveratrol as unsupported (AMA #35, 2022; #272, 2023).

## Emotional health

- **IF your plan optimizes the body but ignores the mind, THEN it is incomplete — treat emotional health as a full domain.** Attia spends about as much time on emotional health as on exercise (*Outlive*, ch. 17).

## Epistemics and care

- **IF the evidence is presented as certain, THEN distrust it — in biology the best you can do is reduce uncertainty.** Reason in probabilities, not false precision (*Outlive*, ch. 3).
- **IF a decision is about testing, treatment, or medication for a specific person, THEN make it with a physician who has the full picture — the framework supplies questions, not prescriptions.** The corpus does not replace clinical care (*Outlive*, throughout).
