# Frameworks — the Peter Attia corpus

Every named framework in the published corpus, with when-to-use, concrete steps, and source. Sources are *Outlive* (Peter Attia with Bill Gifford, Harmony, 2023) by chapter, named *Drive* episodes, and peterattiamd.com. Study aid; not medical advice; individualized decisions belong with a physician.

## Medicine 3.0 (the operating framework)

*When to use:* As the lens for any longevity decision — it defines how the corpus reasons about health.
*Steps:*
1. **Prevent, don't wait.** Ask what chronic disease you are trying to delay and what its decades-long runway is, rather than waiting for symptoms.
2. **Personalize.** Ask how you resemble or differ from the average trial participant before applying a study's result.
3. **Price the risk honestly** — including the risk of doing nothing, and the harms of the intervention itself.
4. **Weight healthspan equally** with lifespan.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 3.

## The Four Horsemen risk audit

*When to use:* When a user asks "what should I actually worry about?" or wants to prioritize prevention effort.
*Steps:*
1. **Atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease** — measure apoB, check Lp(a) once, manage blood pressure and metabolic health; act early because plaque accrues over decades.
2. **Cancer** — apply the prevention triad: reduce risk (metabolic health, don't smoke, limit alcohol), plan for early and broad screening, know that early detection dominates.
3. **Neurodegenerative disease** — the corpus's main levers are exercise, sleep, metabolic health, and (for those with family history) knowing your genetic risk; there is no drug cure, so prevention is the play.
4. **Metabolic dysfunction** — assess fasting insulin, glucose tolerance, and visceral/ectopic fat; treat this as the soil that worsens the other three.
Prioritize the earliest-acting, highest-leverage levers.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 6-9; Drive #229 (2022) on apoB.

## The Centenarian Decathlon

*When to use:* When a user needs a concrete training goal or doesn't know what "fitness for longevity" means.
*Steps:*
1. **List the ~10 tasks** you want to do in your 80s and 90s (hike with a pack, get off the floor unassisted, lift a suitcase into an overhead bin, carry grandchildren, sustain a brisk walk uphill).
2. **Make each measurable today** (e.g., a target grip strength, a farmer's-carry load, a rucking distance, a VO2 max band).
3. **Back-cast.** Because capacity declines with age, set today's target well above the Marginal-Decade requirement so a surplus remains later.
4. **Train the four pillars** against those targets.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 11; Drive #261 (2023).

## The four pillars of exercise

*When to use:* Structuring a complete training program rather than doing one modality.
*Steps:*
1. **Stability first** — train the foundational control (breathing, foot and grip stability, spine and joint control) that lets you produce force safely and avoid injury.
2. **Strength** — resistance train to build and preserve muscle mass and force; defend it aggressively because it falls with age.
3. **Zone 2 aerobic base** — accumulate multiple hours per week at the highest steady effort you can hold with lactate under ~2 mmol/L (conversational pace) to build mitochondrial density and metabolic flexibility.
4. **VO2 max / zone 5** — add short, hard intervals near maximal aerobic power to raise the ceiling of cardiorespiratory fitness, the corpus's strongest mortality marker.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 11-12; Drive #261 (2023).

## The five tactical domains (ordered plan)

*When to use:* When a user wants a complete, prioritized longevity plan.
*Steps, in order of leverage:*
1. **Exercise** — first and most powerful; run the four pillars and the Decathlon.
2. **Nutrition** — anchor on protein (~1 g/lb body weight) and energy balance; personalize with biomarkers (nutritional biochemistry) rather than a named diet; use CR/DR/TR as optional tools.
3. **Sleep** — protect ~7-9 hours as non-negotiable; fix quality and consistency before reaching for aids.
4. **Emotional health** — treat distress tolerance, relationships, and meaning as a full domain; the corpus spends about as much time here as on exercise.
5. **Exogenous molecules** — drugs, hormones, supplements last, graded by evidence, chosen with a physician.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 3, 11-17.

## Nutritional biochemistry (the nutrition framework)

*When to use:* Any eating decision; replaces "which diet."
*Steps:*
1. **Set the goal** in metabolic terms (improve insulin sensitivity, preserve muscle, adjust body composition), not weight alone.
2. **Fix the load-bearing levers** — adequate protein and appropriate energy balance.
3. **Personalize with data** — use tools like a continuous glucose monitor and periodic lipid/metabolic panels to see how specific foods affect *your* biomarkers.
4. **Choose an intake strategy** among CR (eat less), DR (cut a category), TR (compress the window) that fits your data — none is mandatory, and watch fasting's muscle-loss cost.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 15-16.

## Cardiovascular risk workup (lipid-centric)

*When to use:* Assessing and acting on heart-disease risk — the corpus's number-one Horseman.
*Steps:*
1. **Measure apoB**, not just LDL-C, to count atherogenic particles directly.
2. **Check Lp(a) at least once** in a lifetime, since it is largely genetic and, if high, changes how aggressively you manage everything else.
3. **Act early and hold** — lower apoB in your 30s-40s rather than waiting, because plaque compounds over decades.
4. **Bring this to your physician** — testing and any medication are clinical decisions, not self-prescribed.
*Source:* Drive #229 (2022); *Outlive*, ch. 7.

## Cancer prevention triad

*When to use:* Reasoning about the cancer Horseman.
*Steps:*
1. **Prevent** — pursue metabolic health, avoid smoking, limit alcohol, maintain healthy body composition.
2. **Detect early and broadly** — screen earlier and more aggressively than default guidelines for informed patients (e.g., the corpus favors earlier colonoscopy), because most cancers are far more survivable when caught early.
3. **Treat smart** — favor newer, targeted therapies that exploit cancer's specific vulnerabilities.
Weigh screening's own harms (false positives, overdiagnosis) with a physician.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 8; Drive #391 (2025).

## Exogenous-molecule triage

*When to use:* When a user is considering a longevity drug, hormone, or supplement.
*Steps:*
1. **Confirm the base is handled** — exercise, nutrition, sleep, emotional health first; molecules are the fifth domain, not a shortcut.
2. **Grade by evidence** — the corpus rates rapamycin as promising-but-unproven-in-humans, metformin and fasting as uncertain, resveratrol as unsupported; be skeptical of hype.
3. **Weigh the downside** — every molecule has risks; require a plausible mechanism and honest risk/benefit.
4. **Decide with a physician** — dosing and prescription are clinical, not self-directed.
*Source:* *Outlive*, ch. 3; Drive AMA #35 (2022); #272 (2023).
