# Sources — the Peter Attia corpus

Complete bibliography with dates. Primary sources (Attia's own book and podcast) first, then supporting episodes and articles. This is the basis for `provenance.sources` in SEAT.md. All citations verified via peterattiamd.com and the podcast's published feed. Study aid; not medical advice; not affiliated with or endorsed by Peter Attia.

## Primary source — the book

- **Attia, Peter, with Bill Gifford. *Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity*. Harmony / Penguin Random House, March 2023.** #1 New York Times bestseller. The central corpus text. Chapter structure referenced throughout this seat:
  - The three-era model of medicine and Medicine 3.0 (ch. 1-3)
  - Healthspan vs. lifespan (ch. 1)
  - The Four Horsemen: metabolic disease / insulin resistance (ch. 6), cardiovascular disease and apoB (ch. 7), cancer and screening (ch. 8), neurodegenerative disease / Alzheimer's (ch. 9)
  - Exercise, the four pillars, Centenarian Decathlon, Marginal Decade (ch. 11-12)
  - Nutrition / nutritional biochemistry, protein, CR/DR/TR (ch. 15-16)
  - Sleep (ch. 16, and via the Matthew Walker episodes)
  - Emotional health (ch. 17)

## Primary source — the podcast (The Peter Attia Drive)

Episodes cited in this seat, with release dates:

- **#217 — "Exercise, VO2 max, and longevity" with Mike Joyner, M.D.** Released Aug 8, 2022. VO2 max as the strongest mortality-linked fitness marker; how it is measured and improved.
- **#229 — "Understanding cardiovascular disease risk, cholesterol, and apoB"** (with Allan Sniderman). Released Oct 31, 2022. Why apoB is a superior cardiovascular risk metric; lipoprotein primer; early intervention.
- **#261 — "Training for The Centenarian Decathlon: zone 2, VO2 max, stability, and strength."** Released Jul 10, 2023. Filmed live for *Outlive* readers; defines the Marginal Decade, the Centenarian Decathlon, back-casting, and the four exercise pillars.
- **#47, #48, #49 — Matthew Walker, Ph.D., on sleep (three-part series).** Released 2019. Dangers of poor sleep, Alzheimer's risk, memory, cardiovascular and metabolic consequences.
- **#126 — Matthew Walker, Ph.D., on sleep, immune function, and more.** Released 2020. Practical sleep and its downstream health effects.
- **#221 — "Understanding sleep and how to improve it."** Released 2022. Sleep architecture and practical improvement.
- **AMA #27 — on VO2 max and mortality.** Released Sep 20, 2021. Discusses Mandsager et al. 2018 (JAMA Network Open): moving from the lowest fitness quartile toward average roughly halves ten-year mortality; low vs. elite fitness ~5x difference.
- **AMA #35 — "'Anti-Aging' Drugs — NAD+, metformin, & rapamycin."** Released 2022. Evidence-grading of exogenous molecules.
- **#272 — "Rapamycin"** with David Sabatini, M.D., Ph.D. and Matt Kaeberlein, Ph.D. Released 2023. Rapamycin's promise and unanswered questions.
- **#334 / related Tom Dayspring episodes — cardiovascular disease, apoB, cholesterol, brain health.** Deep lipidology series supporting the apoB and Lp(a) positions.
- **#391 — "Colorectal cancer screening."** Released 2025. Case for early, aggressive, personalized cancer screening.

## Primary source — articles and newsletter (peterattiamd.com)

- **peterattiamd.com** — Attia's article and newsletter archive (2019-2025), including pieces on VO2 max and all-cause mortality ("The [almost] unbelievable effects of a high maximal aerobic capacity on all-cause mortality"), "How does VO2 max correlate with longevity?", the metformin-and-longevity essay, and the *Outlive* landing page. Source for the verified VO2 max quote and the Mandsager statistics.

## Supporting / corroborating references (secondary; used only to cross-check, not as primary claims)

- **Mandsager, K. et al. (2018).** "Association of Cardiorespiratory Fitness With Long-term Mortality Among Adults Undergoing Exercise Treadmill Testing." *JAMA Network Open*, 1(6):e183605. The underlying study behind the corpus's VO2 max / mortality figures (~122,000 patients).
- Independent book-summary and transcript sources (grahammann.net, probinism.com, podcastnotes.org, podscripts.co) used only to cross-check wording and chapter attribution; all load-bearing claims trace to the primary sources above.
