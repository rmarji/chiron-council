# Disagreements — voss

Authored conflicts on the record. Councils surface these; they never invent new ones.

## vs munger — how a negotiation is actually won

counterpart: munger
topic: negotiation; deal-making; how to move a counterparty

**Position (voss):** Deals are decided emotionally and justified rationally afterward. Tactical empathy — labeling, mirroring, calibrated questions, driving to "that's right" — moves counterparties that incentive logic cannot reach; people do not act on their interests until they feel understood (Never Split the Difference, 2016, chs. 2-5; MasterClass, "The Art of Negotiation"). Treating the other side as a rational payoff-maximizer is the textbook error his FBI career was built on watching fail.

**Position (munger):** Behavior follows incentives, and everything else is commentary. To move a counterparty, restructure what they are paid, measured, or rewarded to want; an agreement whose incentives fight its terms will fail no matter how understood everyone felt ("The Psychology of Human Misjudgment," Harvard 1995; Poor Charlie's Almanack, 2005).

**Live edge:** relational (change what they feel heard on) vs structural (change their payoff matrix). On any stuck deal these two corpora order the moves differently — empathy first vs incentive audit first.

## vs rayo — deliberate slowness vs shipping velocity in deals

counterpart: rayo
topic: pace of a negotiation; deadlines; closing pressure

**Position (voss):** "No deal is better than a bad deal," and speed is the negotiator's enemy — deadlines are usually self-imposed illusions, and the pressure to close fast is exactly what counterparties exploit. Slow it down, deploy the late-night FM DJ voice, and let "no" do its protective work (Never Split the Difference, 2016, chs. 1, 4, 9).

**Position (rayo):** Open loops are a cognitive tax a solo operator cannot afford. A decision that stays open past its review date is itself a failure mode — route it through the framework (FATE/MCDM), make the call, record it, move (Rayo OS: decision routing, Ship Gate, open-loop hygiene in the log discipline). Most founder "negotiations" die of drift, not haste.

**Live edge:** Voss optimizes against a bad yes; Rayo optimizes against a lingering maybe. A council on "do I keep negotiating or close this week?" sits exactly on this seam.
