# Quotes — the Voss corpus

Short attributed excerpts, verified against the sources noted. Paraphrases are explicitly marked. Use sparingly and always with attribution. NSTD = *Never Split the Difference* (Voss with Tahl Raz, HarperBusiness, 2016).

## Verified — Never Split the Difference

- "He who has learned to disagree without being disagreeable has discovered the most valuable secret of negotiation." — NSTD (Ch. 9, "Bargain Hard").
- "Negotiation is not an act of battle; it's a process of discovery." — NSTD.
- "'Yes,' as I always say, is nothing without 'How?'" — NSTD (Ch. 8, "Guarantee Execution").
- "There are actually three kinds of 'Yes': Counterfeit, Confirmation, and Commitment." — NSTD (Ch. 4, "Beware 'Yes' — Master 'No'").
- "'No' is the start of the negotiation, not the end of it." — NSTD (Ch. 4).
- "The last rule of labeling is silence. Once you've thrown out a label, be quiet and listen." — NSTD (Ch. 3, "Don't Feel Their Pain, Label It").
- "Mirrors work magic. Repeat the last three words (or the critical one to three words) of what someone has just said." — NSTD (Ch. 2, "Be a Mirror").
- "The beauty of empathy is that it doesn't demand that you agree with the other person's ideas." — NSTD (Ch. 3).
- "Negotiate in their world. Persuasion is not about how bright or smooth or forceful you are. It's about the other party convincing themselves that the solution you want is their own idea." — NSTD (Ch. 7, "Create the Illusion of Control").
- "Hope is not a strategy." — NSTD.
- "Set boundaries, and learn to take a punch or punch back, without anger. The guy across the table is not the problem; the situation is." — NSTD (Ch. 9).

## Verified — talks, courses, interviews

- "The most dangerous negotiation is the one you don't know you're in." — Voss, recurring line in his MasterClass (2019), keynotes, and Black Swan Group materials.
- "The majority of what I'm doing is mirroring. You can be caught off guard by what the other person is saying, and you just start repeating what they're saying, and they keep talking." — Voss on the 1993 Chase Manhattan Bank robbery negotiation (MasterClass case study, 2019, as reported in coverage of the course).
- On tactical empathy requiring no agreement: Voss states that if sympathy, compassion, and agreement are stripped out of the definition, "you can be empathic with anybody" — Lex Fridman Podcast #364 (2023). (Near-verbatim; the surrounding sentence structure is condensed.)

## Marked paraphrases (faithful to the corpus, not verbatim)

- *Paraphrase:* Aim for "that's right," never settle for "you're right" — "that's right" means they feel understood; "you're right" means they want you to stop talking. — NSTD Ch. 5's central teaching, condensed.
- *Paraphrase:* Compromise is wearing one black shoe and one brown shoe: splitting the difference between two good options produces a bad one. — NSTD Ch. 6's shoe illustration, condensed.
- *Paraphrase:* Deadlines are usually arbitrary, almost always flexible, and hardly ever trigger the consequences we fear. — NSTD Ch. 6 on deadline pressure.
- *Paraphrase:* Every negotiation holds three to five Black Swans — pieces of information that would change everything if surfaced; assume they exist and hunt for them. — NSTD Ch. 10.
- *Paraphrase:* "No" makes people feel safe and in control; that is why it is the beginning of engagement rather than the end. — NSTD Ch. 4.
- *Paraphrase:* Don't feel their pain — label it. Demonstrated understanding beats claimed understanding ("I understand" is worthless). — NSTD Ch. 3.
