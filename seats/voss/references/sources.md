# Sources — the Voss corpus

Complete bibliography, primary sources first. This is the provenance base for the seat; `SEAT.md` frontmatter `provenance.sources` is drawn from this list.

## Primary — books

- **Chris Voss with Tahl Raz, *Never Split the Difference: Negotiating As If Your Life Depended On It*** (HarperBusiness, 2016). The core corpus. Chapters: 1 The New Rules · 2 Be a Mirror · 3 Don't Feel Their Pain, Label It · 4 Beware "Yes" — Master "No" · 5 Trigger the Two Words That Immediately Transform Any Negotiation · 6 Bend Their Reality · 7 Create the Illusion of Control · 8 Guarantee Execution · 9 Bargain Hard · 10 Find the Black Swan · Appendix: Prepare a Negotiation One Sheet.
- **Chris Voss and Steve Shull, *The Full Fee Agent: How to Stack the Odds in Your Favor as a Real Estate Professional*** (Independently published, 2022). Applies tactical empathy to real-estate practice; source for the corpus's later emphasis on detachment from outcome and not chasing.

## Primary — courses and training

- **Chris Voss Teaches the Art of Negotiation**, MasterClass (launched 2019). 18 video lessons including Tactical Empathy; Mirroring; Labeling; Body Language & Speech Patterns; The Accusation Audit; The Value of "No"; Bargaining; Black Swans; and the case-study lesson "Case Study: Chase Manhattan Bank Robbery," which plays the actual 1993 negotiation call recordings.
- **The Black Swan Group** (founded by Voss, 2008) — training materials, negotiation-mastery courses, and *The Edge* newsletter; source of fill-in-the-blank label and calibrated-question templates and the "most dangerous negotiation" line.

## Primary — talks

- **"Never Split the Difference," Talks at Google** (2016). Book-launch-era talk covering mirrors, labels, "no"-oriented questions, and the late-night FM DJ voice.
- **"Never Split the Difference," TEDx University of Nevada** (2019). Condensed statement of tactical empathy and the case against compromise.

## Primary — major interviews and podcasts

- **Lex Fridman Podcast #364: "Chris Voss: FBI Hostage Negotiator"** (March 2023). ~2.5 hours: tactical-empathy definition stripped of sympathy/agreement, proactive listening, mirroring and silence, deactivating negativity, shared-vision framing, hostage cases, and views on political/international negotiation.
- **The Tim Ferriss Show #260: "Chris Voss — The Art of Letting Other People Have Your Way"** (2017). Extended treatment of labels, mirrors, and the FM DJ voice with live demonstrations.
- Numerous recurring appearances expanding the corpus (fireside chats, business podcasts, negotiation keynotes, 2016–present); use only claims that can be tied to a named episode.

## Biographical grounding (context for the field cases)

- Voss served in the FBI 1983–2007; SWAT before crisis negotiation; trained at Quantico and with Scotland Yard and Harvard Law School's negotiation program; became the FBI's lead international kidnapping negotiator (early 2000s); worked roughly 150 kidnapping cases. After retiring he founded The Black Swan Group (2008) and taught negotiation at Georgetown's McDonough School of Business and USC Marshall (NSTD Ch. 1; Black Swan Group bio; MasterClass introduction).
- **Chase Manhattan Bank robbery, Brooklyn, September 30, 1993** — hostage-taking resolved through mirroring and the DJ voice; NSTD Ch. 2's anchor case and the MasterClass case study.
- **Harlem standoff, 1998** — three armed fugitives, resolved in about six hours of one-way labeling through an apartment door, no shots fired (NSTD Ch. 3).
- **Jeffrey Schilling kidnapping, Philippines, 2000** — Abu Sayyaf's Abu Sabaya; source of the "that's right" breakthrough (NSTD Ch. 5).
- **Haiti kidnapping wave, early 2000s** — source of the hidden-deadline (Friday party money) insight (NSTD Ch. 6).
- **Ecuador kidnapping case** — resolved through the victim's wife using calibrated questions (NSTD Ch. 7).
- **William Griffin, Rochester NY, 1981** — the first US case of a hostage killed on deadline; the corpus's caveat case on deadlines (NSTD Ch. 6; not a Voss case).
- **Dwight Watson "Tractor Man" standoff, Washington DC, 2003** — resolved through the counterpart's "religion"/worldview; the Ch. 10 Black Swan case.

## Intellectual influences credited inside the corpus

- **Daniel Kahneman** (with Amos Tversky) — *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011); prospect theory, loss aversion, System 1/System 2 (cited NSTD Ch. 1, Ch. 6).
- **Nassim Nicholas Taleb** — *The Black Swan* (2007); namesake of the unknown-unknowns model and of Voss's firm (credited NSTD Ch. 10).
- **Albert Mehrabian** — the 7-38-55 communication ratio (used NSTD Ch. 8).
- **Jim Camp** — *Start with No* (2002); credited in NSTD Ch. 4 as a forerunner of no-orientation.
- **Mike Ackerman** — ex-CIA kidnapping-response specialist; originator of the Ackerman bargaining system (NSTD Ch. 9).
- **FBI Crisis Negotiation Unit** — the Behavioral Change Stairway Model (active listening → empathy → rapport → influence → behavioral change) (NSTD Ch. 5).
- **Robert Mnookin / Harvard Program on Negotiation** — the sparring context that opens NSTD Ch. 1.
