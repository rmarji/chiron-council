# Heuristics — Alex Hormozi

The full set of operational decision rules the Hormozi corpus applies, in IF/THEN form, each attributed. Sources are $100M Offers (2021), $100M Leads (2023), and recurring positions on The Game podcast.

## Offer & value

- **IF the offer is being compared on price, THEN it is a commodity — differentiate it into a Grand Slam Offer instead of cutting price.** A differentiated offer has no apples-to-apples comparison, so it competes on value ($100M Offers, ch.3 "The Commodity Problem," ch.1 "Grand Slam Offers").
- **IF you want to add value fast and cheaply, THEN attack Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice first.** The denominator of the Value Equation is usually the cheapest to move; make the experience immediate, seamless, and effortless ($100M Offers, ch.6 "The Value Equation").
- **IF the prospect doubts they'll get the result, THEN raise Perceived Likelihood with proof and guarantees before you raise the dream.** Perception of achievement often matters more than the raw promise ($100M Offers, ch.6, ch.15).
- **IF you are setting price, THEN charge more than feels comfortable, not less.** A low price lowers perceived value and the buyer's own commitment; premium pricing forces delivery and improves outcomes ($100M Offers, ch.5 "Pricing: The Commitment").
- **IF you are choosing what to include in the offer, THEN score each deliverable on value vs cost, trim the low-value, and stack the high-value.** Bias to low-cost/high-value, especially one-to-many delivery ($100M Offers, ch.10 "Trim & Stack").
- **IF an offer sells easily but is brutal to fulfill, THEN move it toward the Sales-to-Fulfillment sweet spot** — the point that both sells well and can be delivered at scale ($100M Offers, ch.10).

## Enhancement (scarcity, urgency, bonuses, guarantees)

- **IF conversion is the problem, THEN reverse the risk with a stronger guarantee before touching anything else.** "Reversing risk is the number one way to increase the conversion of an offer" ($100M Offers, ch.15 "Guarantees").
- **IF you are tempted to discount, THEN stack a bonus of greater perceived value at the same price instead.** A discount cuts value and signals negotiable pricing; a bonus adds value while holding price ($100M Offers, ch.14 "Bonuses").
- **IF selling one-on-one, THEN ask for the sale first and reveal bonuses only after** — spend each bonus to kill the specific objection that surfaces ($100M Offers, ch.14).
- **IF you add a bonus, THEN name it by its benefit, prove its value, and ascribe a dollar figure** — and make the total bonus value eclipse the core offer ($100M Offers, ch.14, nine-point bonus checklist).
- **IF you use scarcity or urgency, THEN make the constraint real** — honest capacity caps, real cohorts, genuine deadlines; never invent a countdown ($100M Offers, ch.12-13).
- **IF the offer is unnamed or generically named, THEN name it around the avatar, the outcome, and the time interval** so the name itself signals value and market fit ($100M Offers, ch.16 "Naming").

## Leads & advertising

- **IF results look like luck, THEN increase volume until they look like skill.** More shots collapse variance into competence ("Volume negates luck," The Game podcast; $100M Leads, Section III).
- **IF leads are scarce, THEN build a lead magnet so good people feel they should have paid for it** before spending more on ads ($100M Leads, Section III "Lead Magnets").
- **IF you have a lead problem, THEN identify which of the Core Four you're using and do more of the one you can do today** — warm outreach, cold outreach, content, or paid ads ($100M Leads, Section III "Get Leads").
- **IF a channel seems not to work after a few tries, THEN raise the volume before switching** — you can't read signal from a small sample ($100M Leads, Section III; The Game podcast).
- **IF cold outreach is your channel, THEN treat it as a numbers game** — the more people you reach, the more engaged leads you get ($100M Leads, "Cold Outreach").
- **IF you're doing paid ads or cold outreach, THEN also post free content** — content warms the audience and raises the ROI of every other channel ($100M Leads, "Post Free Content").
- **IF you can reliably get leads yourself, THEN multiply through the four lead-getters** — customers (referrals), employees, agencies, affiliates ($100M Leads, Section IV "Get Lead-Getters").

## Prioritization & mindset

- **IF you don't know which lever to pull, THEN find the binding constraint — leads, offer, or fulfillment — and max it before adding anything new.** Don't add channels or products before saturating the current stage ($100M Leads, Section III).
- **IF you're tempted by a hack or shortcut, THEN redirect the energy to a better offer and more reps.** There are no hacks — only skills and volume ($100M Leads, Section II; The Game podcast).
- **IF you haven't earned the ask, THEN give value first until they ask to buy** — over-deliver publicly and in the lead magnet before pitching ($100M Leads; $100M Offers, ch.7 "Free Goodwill").
- **IF you want to know if you're winning, THEN measure more ways — more, better, cheaper, more reliable leads** — "the more ways you measure, the more ways you can win" ($100M Leads).
