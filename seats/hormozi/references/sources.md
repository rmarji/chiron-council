# Sources — Alex Hormozi

Complete bibliography for this seat, primary sources first. Provenance for every claim in SEAT.md and the reference files traces to an entry here.

## Primary — authored by Alex Hormozi

1. **$100M Offers: How To Make Offers So Good People Feel Stupid Saying No** — Acquisition.com Publishing, 2021 (ISBN 9781737475736). The first volume of the Acquisition.com series and the most-cited source in this seat. Structure used as citation anchors:
   - **Section I — How We Got Here / Grand Slam Offers** (ch.1 "Grand Slam Offers"): the thesis of an offer "so good people feel stupid saying no."
   - **Section II — Pricing** (ch.3 "The Commodity Problem," ch.4 "Finding The Right Market — A Starving Crowd," ch.5 "Pricing: The Commitment"): differentiate out of commodity competition; pick a starving market; charge premium prices.
   - **Section III — Value: Create Your Offer** (ch.6 "The Value Equation," ch.7 "Free Goodwill," ch.8 "The Thought Process Behind The Offer," ch.9 "Create Your Offer: Problems & Solutions" incl. the Delivery Cube, ch.10 "Create Your Offer: Trim & Stack" incl. the value/cost 2×2 and the Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum).
   - **Section IV — Enhancing Your Offer** (ch.11 overview, ch.12 "Scarcity," ch.13 "Urgency," ch.14 "Bonuses," ch.15 "Guarantees," ch.16 "Naming").

2. **$100M Leads: How to Get Strangers To Want To Buy Your Stuff** — Acquisition.com Publishing, 2023. The second volume; the source for all lead-generation claims. Structure used as citation anchors:
   - **Section II — Get Understanding** (definitions: a lead is a person you can contact; engaged leads; leads and lead magnets; "more, better, cheaper, reliably").
   - **Section III — Get Leads** (the **Core Four**: warm outreach, post free content, cold outreach, paid ads; **lead magnets**; give-first logic).
   - **Section IV — Get Lead-Getters** (the four lead-getters: customers/referrals, employees, agencies, affiliates).

3. **The Game with Alex Hormozi** — podcast, 2020–present (formerly "The Game w/ Alex Hormozi," previously "Gym Secrets"). Source for recurring maxims and positions that appear across both books, most notably "Volume negates luck," the anti-hack / skills-and-reps stance, and the business-as-machine constraint framing.

4. **Alex Hormozi YouTube channel** — 2020–present. Long-form talks and breakdowns that restate and expand the book frameworks (offers, value equation, guarantees, the Core Four). Used as corroboration for the published book claims, not as an independent source of new frameworks.

5. **Acquisition.com free resources and training** — acquisition.com. Companion materials, worksheets, and training that accompany the books (e.g., the offer and lead-generation checklists). Used to confirm framework structure.

## Citation conventions used in this seat

- $100M Offers claims: "$100M Offers, ch.N" with the chapter title where helpful (e.g., ch.6 "The Value Equation," ch.15 "Guarantees").
- $100M Leads claims: "$100M Leads, Section III" with the sub-section name where helpful (e.g., "Get Leads," "Lead Magnets").
- Recurring positions not tied to a specific page: "The Game podcast."
- Direct quotes appear only in `quotes.md` with verified or near-verbatim wording; everything else in the reference files is paraphrase and is presented as such.
- Where exact chapter numbering varies between print and Kindle editions, the section-level anchor is authoritative and the chapter number is a best-effort locator.

## Provenance and scope notes

- This seat is deliberately scoped to the two published books plus the podcast/YouTube positions, because those are the sources whose frameworks are stated in a stable, citable form. Blog posts, sponsored content, and third-party summaries were used only to locate and cross-check book claims, never as the origin of a framework.
- The subject is living and unlicensed, so this seat runs in **corpus mode**: every claim is attributed to the published work and spoken in the third person; no first-person impersonation and no invented positions on companies or people the corpus does not address.
- Verbatim quotes were verified against publisher and aggregator quote pages for $100M Offers and $100M Leads; anything that could not be confirmed word-for-word is marked as paraphrase in `quotes.md` or omitted, per the corpus-mode rule to omit the unverifiable rather than approximate it.
