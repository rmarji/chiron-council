# Quotes — Naval Ravikant

Verified short excerpts with attribution. Wording checked against the published tweetstorm text and the Almanack compilations; items the corpus states but whose exact wording varies across transcripts are explicitly marked **(paraphrase)**. Use sparingly, always inside quotation marks, always with the citation.

## From the "How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" tweetstorm (May 2018)

- "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep. Money is how we transfer time and wealth. Status is your place in the social hierarchy."
- "You're not going to get rich renting out your time. You must own equity — a piece of a business — to gain your financial freedom."
- "You will get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get. At scale."
- "Pick an industry where you can play long term games with long term people."
- "All the returns in life, whether in wealth, relationships, or knowledge, come from compound interest."
- "Pick business partners with high intelligence, energy, and, above all, integrity."
- "Don't partner with cynics and pessimists. Their beliefs are self-fulfilling."
- "Specific knowledge is knowledge that you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else, and replace you."
- "Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like work to others." **(near-verbatim; minor wording varies across reproductions)**
- "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."
- "Embrace accountability, and take business risks under your own name. Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage."
- "Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication (code and media)."
- "Code and media are permissionless leverage."
- "An army of robots is freely available — it's just packed in data centers for heat and space efficiency."
- "If you can't code, write books and blogs, record videos and podcasts."
- "Set and enforce an aspirational personal hourly rate."
- "Work as hard as you can. Even though who you work with and what you work on are more important than how hard you work."
- "Become the best in the world at what you do. Keep redefining what you do until this is true."
- "Read what you love until you love to read." **(near-verbatim)**
- "There are no get rich quick schemes. That's just someone else getting rich off you."
- "Apply specific knowledge, with leverage, and eventually you will get what you deserve."
- "When you're finally wealthy, you'll realize that it wasn't what you were seeking in the first place."
- "You should be too busy to 'do coffee,' while still keeping an uncluttered calendar." **(near-verbatim)**

## From The Almanack of Naval Ravikant (ed. Eric Jorgenson, 2020)

- "Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want." ("Learning Happiness"; also Knowledge Project #18, 2017)
- "Happiness is what's there when you remove the sense that something is missing in your life." ("Learning Happiness")
- "A fit body, a calm mind, a house full of love. These things cannot be bought — they must be earned." (epigraph; originally a tweet, 2018)
- "If you're evenly split on a difficult decision, take the path more painful in the short term." ("Building Judgment")
- "If you cannot decide, the answer is no." **(near-verbatim; "Building Judgment")**
- "Peace is happiness at rest; happiness is peace in motion." **(near-verbatim; "Learning Happiness")**
- "Leverage is a force multiplier for your judgment." ("Building Judgment")
- "99% of effort is wasted." **(paraphrase of the extended passage on finding the 1% that compounds; "Building Judgment")**
- "Impatience with actions, patience with results." **(near-verbatim; "Building Judgment")**
- "Earn with your mind, not your time." (collected in the Almanack; originally a tweet)
- "Escape competition through authenticity." (chapter heading drawn from the How to Get Rich podcast, 2019)
- "No one can compete with you on being you." **(near-verbatim; "Building Wealth")**
- "Meditation is intermittent fasting for the mind." **(near-verbatim; "Learning Happiness")**
- "Retirement is when you stop sacrificing today for an imaginary tomorrow." ("Learning Happiness"; also discussed on Joe Rogan Experience #1309, 2019)

## From podcasts and interviews

- The rich-and-anonymous preference: the desirable end state is being wealthy without being famous — fame is a liability, wealth a tool. **(paraphrase; Joe Rogan Experience #1309, June 2019)**
- The salary-addiction warning: a monthly salary is habit-forming and is what keeps capable people renting out their time. **(paraphrase; the "heroin and a monthly salary" framing appears in the How to Get Rich podcast series, 2019)**
- "Happiness is a skill you develop and a choice you make." **(paraphrase; recurring formulation — Knowledge Project #18, 2017; Tim Ferriss Show #473, 2020)**
- The principal-agent compression, quoting Julius Caesar: "If you want it done, then go. If not, then send." ("Principal-Agent Problem," How to Get Rich podcast, 2019)
- The four kinds of luck — blind luck, luck from hustle, luck from awareness, luck from character — with the fourth as the buildable one. **(framework summary; "Making Money Isn't About Luck," How to Get Rich podcast, 2019; typology after James Austin, Chase, Chance, and Creativity, 1978)**
- Wealth as applied knowledge, and optimism about its unbounded creation. **(theme summary; Naval Podcast, "The Beginning of Infinity" Parts 1–2 with Brett Hall, 2021, discussing David Deutsch)**

## Attribution notes

- The Almanack is a compilation Jorgenson assembled from Naval's tweets, essays, and interviews with his approval; where a line predates the book, the earlier source is noted.
