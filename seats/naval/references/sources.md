# Sources — Naval Ravikant

Complete bibliography for this seat, primary sources first. Provenance for every claim in SEAT.md and the reference files traces to an entry here.

## Primary — authored by Naval Ravikant

1. **"How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" tweetstorm** — twitter.com/naval, May 31, 2018. The canonical compression of the wealth thread: wealth vs money vs status, specific knowledge, accountability, the leverage taxonomy (labor, capital, code/media), permissionless leverage, long-term games, aspirational hourly rate, get-rich-quick refusal. The single most-cited source in this seat.
2. **How to Get Rich podcast series** — Naval Ravikant with Babak Nivi, nav.al (2019). Episode-by-episode expansion of the tweetstorm; the source for episode-level citations used throughout ("Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status," "Escape Competition Through Authenticity," "Play Long-Term Games With Long-Term People," "Arm Yourself With Specific Knowledge," "Learn to Sell, Learn to Build," "Take Accountability to Earn Equity," "Fortunes Require Leverage," "Product and Media Are New Leverage," "Set an Aspirational Hourly Rate," "Productize Yourself," "There Are No Get Rich Quick Schemes," "Making Money Isn't About Luck," "Principal-Agent Problem," "Kelly Criterion: Avoid Ruin"). Transcripts published at nav.al.
3. **nav.al blog essays** — including "Life Formulas I" (2008; the early formula sketches: happiness, health, and wealth decomposed into inputs), "Seek Wealth" and the other tweetstorm-derived posts (2019–2020), and "A Calm Mind, a Fit Body, a House Full of Love" (2020).
4. **Naval Podcast, "The Beginning of Infinity," Parts 1–2 with Brett Hall** (2021). Naval's engagement with David Deutsch's epistemology: knowledge creation as unbounded, wealth as applied knowledge, fallibilism, and rational optimism. Source for the seat's epistemic-optimism claims.
5. **Twitter/X corpus, @naval** (2007–present). Individual tweets cited where noted (e.g., "A fit body, a calm mind, a house full of love," 2018; "Earn with your mind, not your time"; "Meditation is intermittent fasting for the mind").

## Primary-adjacent — compiled with approval

6. **Eric Jorgenson, The Almanack of Naval Ravikant: A Guide to Wealth and Happiness** (Magrathea, 2020). Free-to-read compilation of Naval's tweets, essays, and interview transcripts, organized into a wealth half ("Building Wealth," "Building Judgment") and a happiness half ("Learning Happiness," "Saving Yourself," "Philosophy"). Section names are used as citation anchors throughout this seat. Foreword by Tim Ferriss.

## Major interviews and podcast appearances

7. **The Knowledge Project #18, "Naval Ravikant: The Angel Philosopher"** — with Shane Parrish, Farnam Street (2017). Reading and foundations, happiness as a trainable skill, desire as suffering, decision-making, honesty as a core practice.
8. **Joe Rogan Experience #1309** — June 2019. The mainstream two-hour synthesis: wealth creation vs status games, rich-and-anonymous, redefining retirement, social media and outrage economics, desire and peace.
9. **The Tim Ferriss Show #97, "The Evolutionary Angel"** (August 2015) — early long-form interview: angel investing, habits, reading, non-consensus decision-making.
10. **The Tim Ferriss Show #473** (October 2020) — the happiness-forward follow-up: reducing anxiety, meditation practice, desire audits, and the inner game that the Almanack's second half compiles.

## Intellectual antecedents the corpus explicitly draws on

11. **James Austin, Chase, Chance, and Creativity** (1978) — origin of the four-kinds-of-luck typology that the How to Get Rich podcast adapts.
12. **Jason Roberts, "How to Increase Your Luck Surface Area"** (codusoperandi, c. 2010) — the "Luck = Doing x Telling" formulation adopted in the luck discussion.
13. **Nassim Nicholas Taleb — Skin in the Game** (2018) and the broader Incerto — accountability, ruin avoidance, and practitioner trust; cited approvingly across the podcast series.
14. **David Deutsch, The Beginning of Infinity** (2011) — the epistemology behind the Brett Hall episodes: problems are soluble, knowledge creation is unbounded.
15. **Benjamin Franklin, Poor Richard's Almanack** (1732–1758) — the explicit model for Jorgenson's compilation format.

## Citation conventions used in this seat

- Tweetstorm claims: "(tweetstorm, May 2018)" or "(tweetstorm, 2018)".
- Podcast-series claims: episode title + "How to Get Rich podcast, 2019".
- Book claims: "Almanack" + section name ("Building Wealth," "Building Judgment," "Learning Happiness").
- Interview claims: show + episode number + year.
- Ideas Naval adopted from others (luck typology, luck surface area, skin in the game, Deutschian optimism) are attributed to their originators at first use.
- Direct quotes appear only in `quotes.md`-verified wording; everything else is paraphrase.
