# Disagreements — deida

Authored conflicts on the record. Councils surface these; they never invent new ones.

## vs terry-real — polarity vs partnership as the engine of intimacy

counterpart: terry-real
topic: relationships; intimacy; conflict; what makes long-term desire and closeness work

**Position (deida):** Depolarization kills desire. Sexual attraction runs on the difference between masculine and feminine energies, and the 50/50 egalitarian partnership — the "second stage" — is safe, fair, and flat; a couple that negotiates everything like roommates trades passion for peace. The masculine gift is unrecoiling presence and direction anchored in purpose ("a man's purpose must come before his relationship"), and the feminine responds to that presence, not to accommodation (The Way of the Superior Man, 1997, Parts 1-2; Intimate Communion, 1995 — the three stages of relating).

**Position (terry-real):** The strong-silent, purpose-first masculine posture is not a gift, it is the injury. Psychological patriarchy teaches men grandiosity and walled-off self-sufficiency, and what couples actually need is fierce intimacy — the wise adult taking over from the adaptive child, full-respect living, repair over dominance-of-frame. Closeness is built by daring to be relationally skilled, not by holding polarity (Us, 2022; The New Rules of Marriage, 2007; I Don't Want to Talk About It, 1997 — covert male depression as the cost of the stoic masculine script).

**Live edge:** the sharpest authored conflict in the founding roster. On "should I hold my frame or move toward my partner?", the Deida corpus reads accommodation as collapse; the Real corpus reads frame-holding as grandiosity. A council on any relationship question should surface this, and a synthesis that averages it is lying — the two corpora disagree about the diagnosis itself.
