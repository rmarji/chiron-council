# Sources — David Deida

Complete bibliography with dates. Primary sources first. Several titles were first issued by Plexus (Deida's own imprint) or Health Communications and later reissued by Sounds True, so some titles carry two legitimate dates; both are given where known.

## Primary — books

- **The Way of the Superior Man: A Spiritual Guide to Mastering the Challenges of Women, Work, and Sexual Desire.** Plexus, 1997; Sounds True edition, 2004; 20th-anniversary edition with a new preface, Sounds True, 2017. The corpus's center of gravity: eight parts (A Man's Way; Dealing with Women; Working with Polarity and Energy; What Women Really Want; Your Dark Side; Feminine Attractiveness; Body Practices; Men's and Women's Yoga of Intimacy) of short directive chapters. Source of the purpose-first, edge, presence, and praise teachings cited throughout this seat.
- **Intimate Communion: Awakening Your Sexual Essence.** Health Communications, 1995. Deida's first book and the systematic statement of the model: sexual essence (masculine/feminine as orientations independent of gender), sexual polarity, and the three stages of relationship — dependence, the 50/50 relationship, and intimate communion.
- **It's a Guy Thing: An Owner's Manual for Women.** Health Communications, 1997. Q&A-format companion volume addressed to women, applying the polarity and essence model from the feminine reader's side.
- **Finding God Through Sex: A Spiritual Guide to Ecstatic Loving and Deep Passion for Men and Women.** Plexus, 2002; revised Sounds True edition, 2005. The intimacy-as-spiritual-practice teaching in its fullest book form.
- **Blue Truth: A Spiritual Guide to Life & Death and Love & Sex.** Plexus, 2002; Sounds True edition, 2005. Short lyric-essay chapters on openness, fear, giving one's gift, and the masculine/feminine metaphysics (consciousness and light).
- **Wild Nights: Conversations with Mykonos about Passionate Love, Extraordinary Sex, and How to Open to God.** Plexus, 2000; Sounds True edition, 2005. Narrative teaching-tale form; the spiritual-friendship frame around the same practices.
- **Dear Lover: A Woman's Guide to Men, Sex, and Love's Deepest Bliss.** Sounds True, 2005. Written to the feminine-essence reader; source for the feminine-as-radiance and trustable-presence teachings.
- **The Enlightened Sex Manual: Sexual Skills for the Superior Lover.** Sounds True, 2007. Practice-level companion to the audio program.
- **Instant Enlightenment: Fast, Deep, and Sexy.** Sounds True, 2007. Earlier version published as Naked Buddhism: 39 Ways to Free Your Heart and Awaken to Now (Plexus, 2002). Micro-practice chapters.

## Primary — audio and recorded teaching

- **Enlightened Sex: Finding Freedom and Fullness Through Sexual Union.** Sounds True audio program, 2004. The fullest recorded statement of the sexuality-as-yoga teaching; source for breath/body practice framing.
- **The Teaching Sessions: The Way of the Superior Man.** Sounds True audio, 2005. Deida teaching the book's material in his own spoken voice, with elaborations beyond the text.
- **Deida workshops and recorded talks (1990s–2010s).** Weekend intensives and men's/women's workshops on polarity, presence, and purpose; circulated recordings and transcripts are the origin of workshop aphorisms (e.g., "your woman is not your fault") that do not appear verbatim in the books — treat such lines as reported workshop language unless traced.
- **deida.info** — Deida's official site: biography, book pages, and free chapters; used to verify tables of contents and editions.

## Secondary and critical context

- **Interviews and dialogues at Integral Life** (integrallife.com), where Deida is a listed author/contributor; document his relationship to the Ken Wilber–adjacent integral scene and his stage-1/2/3 language in conversation.
- **Critical commentary.** Deida's polarity model is widely adopted in men's work and relationship coaching, and criticized in clinical and academic circles as gender-essentialist and heteronormative, with concerns that "presence under her storm" language can be misused to dismiss a partner's stated grievances. The seat keeps these critiques visible: the model is prescriptive spiritual teaching, not evidence-based psychology, and pairs best with relational/behavioral frameworks (e.g., Terry Real's Relational Life Therapy, the Gottman research corpus) for accountability, repair, and anything clinical.
