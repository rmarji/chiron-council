# Heuristics — David Deida

The corpus's operational rules in IF/THEN form, each attributed. Deida writes in directives (most chapters of The Way of the Superior Man end with explicit practice instructions), so these track his own phrasing closely; paraphrase is used throughout, quotation marks only where wording is verified.

## Polarity and attraction

- IF attraction has gone flat while communication stays good, THEN suspect stage-2 depolarization before suspecting incompatibility — diagnose where each partner abandoned their core energy (Intimate Communion, 1995; The Way of the Superior Man, Part Three).
- IF the relationship runs on constant negotiation and sameness, THEN reintroduce chosen polarity in intimate time rather than renegotiating logistics — difference is the charge (Intimate Communion, the 50/50 relationship; The Way of the Superior Man, "Your Attraction to the Feminine Is Inevitable").
- IF tempted to solve flatness by regressing to traditional rigid roles, THEN refuse the regression and aim at stage 3 — equality of worth plus conscious polarity (Intimate Communion, three-stage teaching).

## Presence under intensity

- IF a partner is in an emotional storm, THEN stay physically and attentively present, and neither flee, freeze, fix, nor fight (The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two, "Stay with Her Intensity—To a Point").
- IF the instinct is to analyze a partner's emotion or tell them to handle it alone, THEN drop the analysis and offer felt presence instead — the request underneath is depth, not logic (The Way of the Superior Man, "Don't Analyze Your Woman"; "Don't Suggest That a Woman Fix Her Own Emotional Problem").
- IF a complaint's literal content keeps changing while the fight repeats, THEN respond to the bid for presence underneath rather than litigating the words (The Way of the Superior Man, Part Four, "Her Complaint Is Content-Free"; "What She Wants Is Not What She Says").
- IF a partner says something extreme mid-storm, THEN read it as the truth of the moment, not testimony or contract (The Way of the Superior Man, "Women Are Not Liars").
- IF intensity crosses into abuse or contempt, THEN stop treating it as a test to pass — presence is not endurance; address it directly and route safety issues to professionals (The Way of the Superior Man, "Stay with Her Intensity—To a Point"; corpus limit).

## Purpose and direction

- IF life is organized around the relationship or a partner's approval, THEN re-establish purpose as first priority and bring fullness, not need, to the relationship (The Way of the Superior Man, "Your Purpose Must Come Before Your Relationship").
- IF the deepest purpose is unknown, THEN make discovering it the immediate task rather than drifting in productive busyness (The Way of the Superior Man, "If You Don't Know Your Purpose, Discover It, Now"; "Don't Get Lost in Tasks and Duties").
- IF a decision is being deferred until an authority figure approves, THEN act from your own deepest knowing — the corpus's directive is to live as if the permission-giver were gone ("Live as If Your Father Were Dead," The Way of the Superior Man, Part One).
- IF a life chapter or career mission has expired, THEN be willing to change everything rather than defending the dead form (The Way of the Superior Man, "Be Willing to Change Everything in Your Life").

## Decisions and integrity

- IF a partner pushes back on a decision, THEN run the motive test: change course only if a genuinely deeper truth was revealed, never to appease or purchase peace (The Way of the Superior Man, "Never Change Your Mind Just to Please a Woman"; "Always Hold to Your Deepest Realization").
- IF holding a decision under pressure, THEN hold it warmly — steadiness delivered coldly becomes punishment and fails the presence test (The Way of the Superior Man, Part Two, synthesis).
- IF chronically deferring decisions to a partner who is asking for direction, THEN own the call after taking counsel — habitual "whatever you want" is abdication, not generosity (The Way of the Superior Man, "Don't Force the Feminine to Make Decisions").

## Growth and the edge

- IF fear is the only reason for waiting, THEN lean just beyond the edge and act now — fear does not resolve first ("In any given moment, a man's growth is optimized if he leans just beyond his edge, his capacity, his fear," The Way of the Superior Man, "Lean Just Beyond Your Edge").
- IF performing courage beyond real capacity, THEN retreat to the honest edge before leaning past it — faked edges collapse under pressure (The Way of the Superior Man, "Know Your Real Edge and Don't Fake It").
- IF waiting for tasks to complete before living fully, THEN stop — nothing completes; engage open-hearted amid the incompletion (The Way of the Superior Man, "Stop Hoping for a Completion of Anything in Life").
- IF friends' criticism stings, THEN treat honest challenge from other men as fuel — the masculine grows by challenge (The Way of the Superior Man, "Enjoy Your Friends' Criticism"; "Praise Her").

## Growing a partner (and being grown)

- IF wanting a feminine-essence partner to change, THEN praise the quality you want more of instead of criticizing the deficit — "the masculine grows by challenge, but the feminine grows by praise" (The Way of the Superior Man, "Praise Her").
- IF silently tolerating an irritation, THEN address it cleanly and soon — tolerance compounds into resentment (The Way of the Superior Man, "Tolerating Her Leads to Resenting Her").
- IF hoping a partner will simply get easier, THEN drop the hope and grow capacity instead — the practice is yours (The Way of the Superior Man, "Stop Hoping for Your Woman to Get Easier").

## Scope limits (the corpus's own and the seat's)

- IF the situation involves abuse, coercion, addiction, or clinical-level distress, THEN suspend polarity practice and refer to licensed professionals — the corpus is prescriptive spiritual teaching, not therapy (seat policy; consistent with the corpus's stated limits).
- IF a teaching is being used to close the heart — entitlement, stonewalling, scorekeeping — THEN it is being misused by the corpus's own standard: every practice must give more love, not less (The Way of the Superior Man, "Do It for Love"; "Live with an Open Heart Even If It Hurts").
