# Sources — Richard Rumelt

Complete bibliography with dates. Primary sources first. This is the pool from which `provenance.sources` in SEAT.md is drawn.

## Books (primary corpus)

- Richard P. Rumelt, **Good Strategy Bad Strategy: The Difference and Why It Matters** (Crown Business, 2011). The core text: the kernel (diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent action); the four hallmarks of bad strategy (fluff, failure to face the challenge, mistaking goals for strategy, bad strategic objectives); why bad strategy is common (unwillingness to choose, template-style planning, New Thought); and the nine-plus sources of power — leverage, proximate objectives, chain-link systems, design, focus, growth, advantage, dynamics, inertia and entropy — plus the "thinking like a strategist" chapters (science of strategy, using your head, keeping your head).
- Richard P. Rumelt, **The Crux: How Leaders Become Strategists** (PublicAffairs, 2022). Challenge-based strategy; gnarly problems; the crux (climbing metaphor); Addressable Strategic Challenges (importance × addressability); collect-cluster-filter diagnosis; the Strategy Foundry process; the case against starting strategy from goals, missions, or financial targets.
- Richard P. Rumelt, **Strategy, Structure, and Economic Performance** (Division of Research, Harvard Business School, 1974; revised edition, Harvard Business School Press, 1986). His doctoral work: classification of diversification strategies and the finding that related-constrained diversifiers outperformed conglomerates — the empirical root of his firm-specific view of advantage.

## Academic articles

- Richard P. Rumelt, **"Diversification Strategy and Profitability,"** Strategic Management Journal, vol. 3, no. 4 (1982). Refinement of the 1974 diversification-performance findings.
- Richard P. Rumelt, **"Towards a Strategic Theory of the Firm,"** in R. B. Lamb (ed.), *Competitive Strategic Management* (Prentice-Hall, 1984). Introduces **isolating mechanisms** — firm-level barriers to imitation (causal ambiguity, reputation, switching costs, patents) — and the view of firms as bundles of idiosyncratic resources earning entrepreneurial rents.
- Richard P. Rumelt, **"Theory, Strategy, and Entrepreneurship,"** in D. J. Teece (ed.), *The Competitive Challenge* (Ballinger, 1987). Entrepreneurial rents and the economics of new strategies.
- Richard P. Rumelt, **"How Much Does Industry Matter?"** Strategic Management Journal, vol. 12, no. 3 (1991), pp. 167-185. Variance decomposition of FTC Line of Business data: stable business-unit effects dominate stable industry effects on profitability (roughly 46% vs. 8%), with corporate-parent effects near zero; a direct rebuttal to Schmalensee (1985).
- Richard P. Rumelt, **"Inertia and Transformation,"** in C. A. Montgomery (ed.), *Resource-Based and Evolutionary Theories of the Firm* (Kluwer, 1995). The theory of organizational inertia later popularized in GSBS ch. 14.
- Rumelt, Schendel, and Teece (eds.), **Fundamental Issues in Strategy: A Research Agenda** (Harvard Business School Press, 1994). Field-defining edited volume; context for his academic standing.

## Essays and articles for practitioners

- Richard P. Rumelt, **"Strategy in a 'structural break,'"** McKinsey Quarterly (December 2008). Written into the financial crisis: structural breaks make incumbent formulas obsolete; posture of flexibility, cash, and selective attack.
- Richard P. Rumelt, **"The perils of bad strategy,"** McKinsey Quarterly (June 2011). Condenses GSBS chs. 3-4: the four hallmarks and root causes of bad strategy; the template-strategy and New Thought critiques.

## Lectures, interviews, podcasts

- **"Good Strategy/Bad Strategy: the difference and why it matters,"** public lecture, London School of Economics (September 2011). Recorded; the most compact spoken statement of the GSBS argument.
- **"Strategy's strategist: An interview with Richard Rumelt,"** McKinsey Quarterly (November 2007), interview by Dan Lovallo and Lenny Mendonca. Strategy work as episodic problem-solving; skepticism of annual planning rituals.
- **"Richard Rumelt on what separates good strategy from bad,"** Inside the Strategy Room podcast, ep. 134, McKinsey (September 2022). The Crux material in interview form.
- **"Good Strategy, Bad Strategy,"** Lenny's Podcast interview with Richard Rumelt (2024). Application of the kernel and crux to product and tech-company strategy.

## Citation practice for this seat

- Book claims cite title + chapter (e.g., "GSBS ch. 7"); article claims cite title + year; spoken claims cite venue + year.
- Frameworks and ideas are not copyrightable; expression is. This seat paraphrases and cites; verbatim excerpts are confined to `quotes.md`, kept short, and attributed.
- Where the corpus is silent (e.g., a domain Rumelt never wrote about), the seat says so rather than extrapolating in his name.
