# Sources — Daniel Kahneman

Complete bibliography for this seat, primary sources first. Every claim in SEAT.md and the reference files traces to an entry here. Daniel Kahneman (1934-2024) was a psychologist at Princeton and Hebrew University, co-founder with Amos Tversky of the heuristics-and-biases program, and recipient of the 2002 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences.

## Primary — books

1. **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011). The synthesis of a career: the two-system architecture (Parts 1, "Two Systems"), the heuristics and biases (Part 2), overconfidence and the illusion of validity (Part 3), prospect theory and choices (Part 4, "Choices"), and the experiencing/remembering selves (Part 5, "Two Selves"). The single most-cited source in this seat. Chapter anchors used throughout: ch. 3 "The Lazy Controller," ch. 5 "Cognitive Ease," ch. 7 "A Machine for Jumping to Conclusions" (WYSIATI), ch. 9 "Answering an Easier Question" (substitution), chs. 11-13 (anchoring, availability), chs. 14-16 (representativeness, base rates, Linda), ch. 17 "Regressions to the Mean," ch. 18 "Taming Intuitive Predictions," ch. 19 (hindsight), ch. 20 "The Illusion of Validity," ch. 21 "Intuitions vs. Formulas," chs. 22-23 (expert intuition, the outside view), ch. 24 "The Engine of Capitalism" (premortem), chs. 26-34 (prospect theory, endowment, fourfold pattern, frames), chs. 35-38 (two selves, peak-end, focusing illusion), and the Conclusions.

2. **Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment** — Daniel Kahneman, Olivier Sibony, and Cass R. Sunstein (Little, Brown Spark / Hachette, May 18, 2021). The late-career work on the *variability* of judgment. Structure used as citation anchors: Part I (finding noise; bias vs. noise; the shooting-range analogy), Part II (system noise = level noise + pattern noise; occasion noise), Part III-IV (how noise happens; the psychology of judgment), Part V ("Decision Hygiene," the noise audit, the mediating assessments protocol, ch. 25 "A Debiased Structured Approach"). Source for every noise-vocabulary claim in this seat.

## Primary — foundational papers

3. **"Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases"** — Amos Tversky and Daniel Kahneman, *Science* 185(4157), pp. 1124-1131 (September 27, 1974). The founding paper: introduces the three heuristics — representativeness, availability, anchoring-and-adjustment — and their signature biases (base-rate neglect, insensitivity to sample size, conjunction, anchoring). Cited as "(Science, 1974)" throughout.

4. **"Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk"** — Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky, *Econometrica* 47(2), pp. 263-292 (March 1979). The most-cited paper in the social sciences on decision under risk: reference dependence, the value function (concave for gains, convex for losses, steeper for losses), loss aversion, and the probability-weighting function. Cited as "(Prospect Theory, 1979)" or "(Econometrica, 1979)."

5. **"The Framing of Decisions and the Psychology of Choice"** — Amos Tversky and Daniel Kahneman, *Science* 211(4481), pp. 453-458 (January 30, 1981). The framing paper: the "Asian disease" problem and the demonstration that logically equivalent gain/loss frames reverse choices. Cited as "(Science, 1981)."

6. **"Anomalies: The Endowment Effect, Loss Aversion, and Status Quo Bias"** — Daniel Kahneman, Jack L. Knetsch, and Richard H. Thaler, *Journal of Economic Perspectives* 5(1), pp. 193-206 (Winter 1991). The mug experiments establishing the endowment effect as a consequence of loss aversion. Cited as "(JEP, 1991)."

## Primary — the Nobel lecture

7. **"Maps of Bounded Rationality: A Perspective on Intuitive Judgment and Choice"** — Daniel Kahneman, Nobel Prize lecture, Aula Magna, Stockholm University (December 8, 2002; published in revised form in *American Economic Review* 93(5), 2003). The retrospective that frames the whole program through *accessibility* and casts System 1 as having the operating characteristics of perception. Cited as "(Nobel lecture, 2002)."

## Related work by the corpus (context, cited where noted)

8. **"Conditions for Intuitive Expertise: A Failure to Disagree"** — Daniel Kahneman and Gary Klein, *American Psychologist* 64(6), pp. 515-526 (2009). The "adversarial collaboration" that sets the conditions under which intuition is valid (regular environment + prolonged practice with feedback); the backbone of TFaS ch. 22.

9. **"Before You Make That Big Decision..."** — Daniel Kahneman, Dan Lovallo, and Olivier Sibony, *Harvard Business Review* (June 2011). The 12-question decision-quality checklist; a practical bridge from TFaS to organizational debiasing and a precursor to Noise.

## Intellectual context the corpus draws on

10. **Amos Tversky** — Kahneman's lifelong collaborator (d. 1996). Nearly all the foundational work is joint; the Nobel and the books repeatedly credit the partnership. Attribution in this seat to "the corpus" reflects that most claims are Tversky-Kahneman joint work.
11. **Herbert Simon — bounded rationality** — the antecedent the Nobel lecture positions the program against and within.
12. **Gary Klein — naturalistic decision-making / recognition-primed decision** — the pro-intuition counterpart in the 2009 adversarial collaboration.
13. **Richard Thaler — behavioral economics** — co-author on the endowment-effect work; the bridge from prospect theory into economics.

## Citation conventions used in this seat

- Book claims: title + chapter name/number, e.g., "(TFaS, 2011, ch. 23, 'The Outside View')" or "(Noise, 2021, Part V)."
- Paper claims: "(Science, 1974)", "(Econometrica, 1979)", "(Science, 1981)", "(JEP, 1991)", "(Nobel lecture, 2002)."
- Direct quotes appear only with the verified wording in `quotes.md`; everything else in this seat is paraphrase and is presented as such.
- Most foundational claims are joint Tversky-Kahneman work; "the Kahneman corpus" is used as shorthand for the published body of that work as compiled and extended in Kahneman's own books.
- Note on replication: several priming and ego-depletion results referenced in TFaS did not replicate, and the corpus acknowledged over-reliance on underpowered studies; the robust core (framing, loss aversion, base-rate neglect, anchoring, the outside view, noise) is weighted accordingly. See `anti-patterns.md`.
