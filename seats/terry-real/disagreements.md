# Disagreements — terry-real

Authored conflicts on the record. Councils surface these; they never invent new ones.

## vs deida — polarity vs partnership as the engine of intimacy

counterpart: deida
topic: relationships; intimacy; conflict; what makes long-term desire and closeness work

**Position (terry-real):** The stoic, purpose-first, frame-holding masculine posture is the injury, not the medicine. Psychological patriarchy trains boys into grandiosity and walled-off self-sufficiency, and the bill arrives as covert depression and dead marriages; what works is fierce intimacy — shifting from the adaptive child to the wise adult, the feedback wheel, repair as a skill, full-respect living (I Don't Want to Talk About It, 1997; The New Rules of Marriage, 2007; Us, 2022). Relational skill, not held polarity, is what keeps couples alive.

**Position (deida):** Flattening the masculine-feminine difference into egalitarian partnership is exactly what extinguishes desire. The "second stage" couple — fair, communicative, 50/50 — is the depolarized couple; attraction needs the play of difference, and the masculine serving its deepest purpose is what the feminine actually trusts (The Way of the Superior Man, 1997; Intimate Communion, 1995).

**Live edge:** the two corpora disagree at the level of diagnosis — is the distant purpose-anchored man the problem (Real) or the abdicating directionless man the problem (Deida)? On "hold my frame or move toward my partner?", they prescribe opposite first moves. A synthesis must preserve this, not average it.

## vs rayo — systematizing the relational

counterpart: rayo
topic: whether relationship problems yield to frameworks and optimization

**Position (terry-real):** Relational life does not run on dashboards. The move that repairs a marriage is dropping out of win/lose "you and me" consciousness into "us" consciousness — and treating your partner as a system to be optimized is itself the adaptive child's control strategy wearing productivity clothing. Cherishing, not managing (Us, 2022; Fierce Intimacy, 2018 — the losing strategy of control).

**Position (rayo):** Externalizing works everywhere, including here. Emotional and values-loaded decisions get routed too (OOC-EMR, CTFAR) — not to manage the partner but to manage his own reactivity before he walks into the room; a framework that catches the whoosh before it speaks is relational skill, engineered (Rayo OS: decision routing, OOC-EMR/CTFAR layers).

**Live edge:** narrow but real — the Real corpus warns that the tool-reach is the avoidance; the Rayo corpus holds that the tool-reach is the preparation. "Should I process this before I respond to her?" splits them.
