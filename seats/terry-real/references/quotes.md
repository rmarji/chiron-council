# Quotes — the Terry Real corpus

Short verified excerpts, each attributed to its source. Wording checked against published editions and author-verified postings; items marked **[paraphrase]** capture a claim faithfully but are not verbatim. Use sparingly and always with attribution.

## From *I Don't Want to Talk About It* (Scribner, 1997)

- "Family pathology rolls from generation to generation like a fire in the woods taking down everything in its path until one person in one generation has the courage to turn and face the flames. That person brings peace to their ancestors and spares the children that follow." (Real identifies this as his most-quoted line; he has also published it with "family dysfunction rolls down from generation to generation" — both variants appear in his own materials.)
- "The flight from shame into grandiosity lies at the heart of male covert depression."
- "Those who do not turn to face their pain are prone to impose it."
- "An addict needs shame like a man dying of thirst needs salt water."
- "Good parenting requires three elements: nurturing, limit setting, and guidance."

## From *How Can I Get Through to You?* (Scribner, 2002)

- "Strength is not the absence of vulnerability."
- "Love is not for the faint of heart."
- "Sustaining relationships with others requires a good relationship to ourselves."
- "As women shut down their needs, they also shut down their sense of pleasure."

## From *The New Rules of Marriage* (Ballantine, 2007)

- "Use 'and,' never 'but.' 'But' cancels out whatever came before it."
- "I invite you to use Janet Hurley's feedback wheel." (Attribution line for the feedback wheel's origin.)
- Objective reality has no place in personal relationships; the question is never who is right but what will help you feel close again. **[paraphrase]** — recurring thesis of the book's treatment of the first losing strategy.
- "What can I give you to help you give me what I want?" — the empowerment formula (winning strategy four).

## From *Us* (Goop Press/Rodale, 2022)

- "Everyone gets to go crazy in long-term relationships, but you have to take turns."
- "There is nothing that harshness does that loving firmness doesn't do better."
- "Maturity comes when we tend to our inner children and don't inflict them" on those we love.
- "We all marry our unfinished business. We all marry our mothers and fathers."
- "You have to help your partner come through for you."
- The adaptive child sees the present through the prism of the past; the whoosh of triggering never stops — what improves is how fast you recover. **[paraphrase]** — core argument of the book's early chapters.

## From *Fierce Intimacy* (Sounds True audio, 2018) and talks

- "You can be right or you can be married." — Real's signature challenge to volatile couples; he uses it across *Fierce Intimacy*, RLT trainings, and interviews, often extended as "…what's more important to you?" (also posted verbatim by the Relational Life Institute). Note: the line is occasionally credited to Esther Perel in secondary sources; Real's own use in his published audio and social channels is primary.
- There is no such thing as constructive criticism in a love relationship. **[paraphrase of a recurring spoken formulation]** — appears throughout his talks and teaching on full-respect living; the book-form treatment of the idea is in *The New Rules of Marriage* (2007).
- Intimacy is not a steady state you achieve; it is a rhythm of harmony, disharmony, and repair, and the skill that matters is repair. **[paraphrase]** — thesis of *Fierce Intimacy* (2018) and *Us* (2022).

## Usage rules for this seat

- Quote only what appears above; never synthesize a "Terry Real quote."
- Keep excerpts short (a sentence or two) and always name the book or talk.
- When the corpus's position is needed in flowing prose, paraphrase and cite rather than quoting at length (fair-use discipline; expression is protected even where ideas are not).
