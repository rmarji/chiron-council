# Sources — the Taleb corpus (the Incerto)

Complete bibliography with dates. Primary sources (Taleb's own books and essays) first, then supporting/secondary material used to verify wording and chapter attributions. The seat draws exclusively on the Incerto and Taleb's own essays for its claims; secondary sources were used only to cross-check quotes and structure.

## Primary — the Incerto (books)

1. **Fooled by Randomness: The Hidden Role of Chance in Life and in the Markets** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. Texere, 2001; 2nd expanded ed., Random House, 2004. *Themes cited:* luck vs skill, survivorship bias, silent evidence, alternative histories / Monte Carlo reasoning, the Solon–Croesus warning, noise vs signal.
2. **The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. Random House, 2007; 2nd ed. with the postscript essay "On Robustness and Fragility," 2010. *Themes cited:* the Black Swan triplet (rarity, extreme impact, retrospective predictability), Extremistan vs Mediocristan (ch. 3), the turkey problem (ch. 4), the narrative fallacy (ch. 6), silent evidence (ch. 8), the ludic fallacy (ch. 9), the scandal of prediction (ch. 10), "The Bell Curve, That Great Intellectual Fraud" (ch. 15).
3. **The Bed of Procrustes: Philosophical and Practical Aphorisms** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. Random House, 2010 (expanded 2016). *Themes cited:* the aphoristic compressions of the Incerto's ethics and epistemics; the Procrustes metaphor for forcing reality to fit our models.
4. **Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. Random House, 2012. *Themes cited:* the fragile/robust/antifragile triad and Damocles/Phoenix/Hydra (ch. 2), the opening definition (Prologue), the fragilista and iatrogenics (Book II, ch. 21), the barbell strategy (ch. 11, "Never Marry the Rock Star"), optionality and Thales (ch. 12), convex tinkering / trial and error (ch. 13–15), the Lindy effect (ch. 20, "Time and Fragility"), via negativa (Book VII), hormesis and overcompensation (ch. 2, Book IV), convexity/concavity (ch. 18–19).
5. **Skin in the Game: Hidden Asymmetries in Daily Life** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. Random House, 2018. *Themes cited:* symmetry and risk-transfer ethics (Prologue, ch. 1), the minority rule (ch. 2), rationality and survival (ch. 18, "How to Be Rational about Rationality"), ergodicity and the logic of risk taking (ch. 19), the primacy of the practitioner.

*(Companion, not separately cited but part of the Incerto:* **The Statistical Consequences of Fat Tails**, STEM Academic Press, 2020 — the technical treatment of the fat-tails argument underlying The Black Swan and Antifragile.)*

## Primary — INCERTO essays (Medium / nassimtaleb.org)

6. **"The Most Intolerant Wins: The Dictatorship of the Small Minority"** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb, INCERTO, 2016. The minority-rule / renormalization argument; folded into Skin in the Game, ch. 2.
7. **"The Intellectual Yet Idiot"** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb, INCERTO, 2016. The IYI archetype; folded into Skin in the Game.
8. **"An Expert Called Lindy"** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb, INCERTO, 2016. The Lindy effect as an expert/filter; expands ch. 20 of Antifragile.

## Secondary — used only to verify wording, chapters, and structure

- **Wikipedia:** entries for *The Black Swan (2007 book)*, *Antifragile (book)*, *Skin in the Game (book)*, *Fooled by Randomness*, *Prospect theory*, *Ludic fallacy*, *Lindy effect* — used to confirm chapter structure, the triad imagery, and the Kahneman/Tversky (1979) Prospect Theory citation.
- **Goodreads / Farnam Street (fs.blog):** aggregated aphorisms from *The Bed of Procrustes* and *Antifragile*, used to cross-check verbatim wording of short quotes.
- **Ergodicity Economics (Ole Peters) writing on probability weighting:** used to characterize the ergodicity-based reinterpretation of Prospect Theory that the corpus endorses (for the Kahneman disagreement).
- **Nat Eliason / Blas.com / williammeller.com book notes** on Antifragile and Skin in the Game: used to cross-check the barbell, via negativa, and ergodicity restatements against the books.
