# Sources — Rayo OS

This seat is **original mode**: self-authored, sourced from Rayo's own operating-system corpus rather than a published bibliography. The corpus is a set of internal documents and skills he maintains; citations throughout this seat name the framework and its home in that corpus (e.g. "FATE, 1-decide skill library"). External thinkers he builds on are listed separately and clearly marked as **influences** — their ideas are adopted and adapted into the OS, not represented as his inventions.

## Primary corpus (self-authored)

- **Operating manual ("me" translation layer)** — the canonical statement of how to work with him: lead-with-the-answer voice rules, one-recommendation decide mode, calibrated pushback with confidence ratings, implicit coaching triggers, expert routing by domain, the standing money filter (via negativa, runway preservation, spend framed as cash / time at a defined hourly baseline / opportunity cost / payback / reversibility), and the self-documented front-of-loop trap.
- **Carapace/ShipOS law set** — the operating spine: Vision → Outcomes → Projects → Tasks altitudes and the laws MAX THREE, SPEC FIRST, EXTERNALIZE, VERIFY, FRESH START, with the SHIP GATE as the emerging sixth. Includes enforcement rules (admission-time prioritization, park-or-kill before adding) and the reliability rationale (running a high-variance engine through externalized state, capped concurrency, and verified outputs).
- **1-decide skill library** (the decision stack, one skill per framework):
  - *route* — the dispatcher; stuck-shape diagnosis table and the one-tool rule.
  - *fate* — Fit / Asymmetry / Timing / Effort scoring, 1–3 each; verdict bands (≥9 pursue, 6–8 park, ≤5 kill), the dimension-at-1 override flag, and the pitfalls (scoring to justify, FATE-as-gate-skip, park-as-bury).
  - *mcdm* — weighted multi-criteria choice; weights-before-scores, sensitivity check, AHP pairwise for close finalists.
  - *ooc-emr* — the values-level layer: Outcomes, Options, Consequences / Evaluate, Mitigate, Resolve.
  - *ctfar* — the emotional layer: Circumstance, Thought, Feeling, Action, Result, plus the analysis-as-avoidance guard.
  - *ship-gate* — the admission gate: told-definition, gate check, told-log schema.
- **4-tell skill library — luck-surface** — the weekly telling practice: untold-doing inventory, signal-over-volume selection, CTA requirement, and inbound review; the operational form of Luck = Doing × Telling.
- **Authorship OS design ("two loops, two valves")** — the chassis: capability loop (Declare, Encode, Externalize, Ship), meaning loop (People, Craft, Renew), Ship Gate and Dividend Policy as the valves, and the apex-is-authorship framing.
- **Decision Log practice** — the append-only log of scored and resolved decisions; the mechanism behind the no-relitigating rule.

## External influences (adopted into the OS, clearly marked)

- **Nassim Nicholas Taleb** — the barbell, optionality and asymmetry (capped loss, uncapped gain), and via negativa (*Antifragile*, 2012; *The Black Swan*, 2007). Feeds the FATE Asymmetry dimension and the default-to-subtraction money filter.
- **Naval Ravikant** — the leverage taxonomy (capital, labor, code, media) and permissionless leverage; luck types and making luck find you (*How to Get Rich* tweetstorm and podcast series, 2018–2020; *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant*, ed. Eric Jorgenson, 2020). Feeds the standing "which leverage applies" question and the luck-surface doctrine.
- **Charlie Munger** — inversion as a first-order habit ("invert, always invert," after Jacobi; *Poor Charlie's Almanack*, 2005). Feeds the pre-mortem step of the reversibility check.
- **Jason Roberts** — luck surface area ("How to Increase Your Luck Surface Area," Codus Operandi essay / TechZing podcast, c. 2010). Origin of Luck = Doing × Telling, adopted as doctrine.
- **Tony Robbins** — the OOC/EMR decision process (*Awaken the Giant Within*, 1991, and workshop material). Adopted as the values-level layer of the decision stack.
- **The CTFAR / self-coaching model** — the Circumstance–Thought–Feeling–Action–Result chain (popularized by Brooke Castillo, The Life Coach School). Adopted as the emotional layer, with his own added guard against analysis-as-avoidance.
- **John von Neumann** — "Probabilistic Logics and the Synthesis of Reliable Organisms from Unreliable Components" (1956). The architecture metaphor for brain-as-compute-not-storage: reliable systems from unreliable parts via external state and boundary checks.
- **Jeff Bezos** — one-way vs two-way doors (Amazon shareholder letters, 1997 onward). Folded into the reversibility check of the compounding lens.
- **Multi-criteria decision analysis / AHP** — standard MCDM practice and Saaty's Analytic Hierarchy Process (Thomas Saaty, 1970s–1980s), adapted into the mcdm skill's weights-first discipline.

## Unreconciled (deliberately not hard-coded)

1. **The fifth law.** FRESH START is canonical in the law set, but a documented proposal would replace it with SCHEDULE ("if it's not scheduled, it's not real"), and the Ship Gate is separately treated as a sixth law and an Authorship OS valve. Multiple versions are live; this seat flags rather than resolves.
2. **The hourly baseline (GHR).** His opportunity-cost rate exists in his private pricing docs and has varied across document generations. This seat references "a defined hourly baseline" throughout and never fixes the number.
3. **Live priorities.** His current MAX THREE changes weekly and is intentionally not embedded; a consult should ask for the current three rather than assume them.
